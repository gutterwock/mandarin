## [phase:atomic] Using 会 (huì) for learned abilities

tags: modal-verbs, ability, hui

The modal verb **会 (huì)** expresses learned abilities or skills — things you know how to do because you learned or practiced them. It answers the question "Can you do X?" where X is something you acquired through learning.

**Structure:** Subject + 会 + Verb + (Object)

**Examples:**
- 我会说汉语。(Wǒ huì shuō Hànyǔ.) — I can speak Chinese.
- 她会开车。(Tā huì kāi chē.) — She can drive.
- 他会做饭。(Tā huì zuò fàn.) — He can cook.

**Negative form:** Use 不会 (bù huì)
- 我不会游泳。(Wǒ bù huì yóu yǒng.) — I can't swim.

## [phase:atomic] Using 可以 (kěyǐ) for permission

tags: modal-verbs, permission, keyi

The modal verb **可以 (kěyǐ)** expresses permission or allowance — whether someone is allowed to do something. It's commonly used to ask for permission or grant/deny it.

**Structure:** Subject + 可以 + Verb + (Object)

**Examples:**
- 我可以进来吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ jìn lái ma?) — May I come in?
- 你可以坐这里。(Nǐ kěyǐ zuò zhèlǐ.) — You may sit here.
- 可以吗？(Kěyǐ ma?) — Is it okay? / May I?

**Negative form:** Use 不可以 (bù kěyǐ)
- 不可以在这里吃东西。(Bù kěyǐ zài zhèlǐ chī dōngxi.) — You can't eat here. / Eating is not allowed here.

## [phase:atomic] Using 要 (yào) for intention

tags: modal-verbs, intention, yao

The modal verb **要 (yào)** expresses intention or desire to do something — what someone wants to do or plans to do. It indicates volition or willingness.

**Structure:** Subject + 要 + Verb + (Object)

**Examples:**
- 我要去学校。(Wǒ yào qù xuéxiào.) — I want to go to school. / I'm going to school.
- 他要喝水。(Tā yào hē shuǐ.) — He wants to drink water.
- 她要买东西。(Tā yào mǎi dōngxi.) — She wants to buy things.

## [phase:atomic] Using 要 (yào) for necessity or advice

tags: modal-verbs, necessity, yao

The modal verb **要 (yào)** also expresses necessity, obligation, or advice — what someone should or must do. Context determines whether 要 means "want to" or "should/must."

**Structure:** Subject + 要 + Verb + (Object)

**Examples:**
- 你要多喝水。(Nǐ yào duō hē shuǐ.) — You should drink more water.
- 我们要学习。(Wǒmen yào xuéxí.) — We need to study.
- 他要去医院。(Tā yào qù yīyuàn.) — He needs to go to the hospital.

**Negative form for "don't want":** Use 不要 (bú yào)
- 我不要这个。(Wǒ bú yào zhège.) — I don't want this.

**Note:** 不想 (bù xiǎng) also means "don't want to," but it uses a different verb (想 = want/think) rather than being the grammatical negative of 要. It's semantically similar but not technically the negative form of 要.

**Negative form for "don't need to":** Use 不用 (búyòng)

## [phase:atomic] Distinguishing 会 from 可以

tags: modal-verbs, comparison, hui-vs-keyi

**会 (huì)** and **可以 (kěyǐ)** both translate to "can" in English, but they have different meanings:

- **会** = learned ability (can do because you learned how)
  - 我会游泳。(Wǒ huì yóu yǒng.) — I can swim (I learned how).

- **可以** = permission or possibility (can do because it's allowed)
  - 我可以游泳。(Wǒ kěyǐ yóu yǒng.) — I can swim / I may swim (it's permitted).

The key distinction: 会 focuses on **skill**, while 可以 focuses on **permission**.

## [phase:atomic] Distinguishing 要 from 可以

tags: modal-verbs, comparison, yao-vs-keyi

**要 (yào)** and **可以 (kěyǐ)** express different aspects of actions:

- **要** = intention, desire, or necessity (want/need/should do)
  - 我要喝水。(Wǒ yào hē shuǐ.) — I want to drink water.

- **可以** = permission or possibility (allowed/permitted to do)
  - 我可以喝水吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ hē shuǐ ma?) — May I drink water?

The key distinction: 要 expresses **your will or need**, while 可以 asks about **what's allowed**.

## [phase:complex] Question forms with modal verbs

tags: modal-verbs, questions, ma-particle

All three modal verbs can form yes/no questions by adding the question particle **吗 (ma)** at the end of the sentence.

**Examples:**
- 你会说英语吗？(Nǐ huì shuō Yīngyǔ ma?) — Can you speak English?
- 我可以坐这里吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ zuò zhèlǐ ma?) — May I sit here?
- 他要去吗？(Tā yào qù ma?) — Does he want to go?

**Answering:**
- Affirmative: Repeat the modal verb — 会 (huì), 可以 (kěyǐ), 要 (yào)
- Negative: Use the negative form — 不会, 不可以, 不要

## [phase:complex] Combining modal verbs with time expressions

tags: modal-verbs, time-expressions, word-order

Modal verbs can combine with time expressions. The typical word order is: Subject + Time + Modal Verb + Verb + (Object)

**Examples:**
- 我明天要去北京。(Wǒ míngtiān yào qù Běijīng.) — I'm going to Beijing tomorrow.
- 他今天不可以来。(Tā jīntiān bù kěyǐ lái.) — He can't come today.
- 她现在会说汉语。(Tā xiànzài huì shuō Hànyǔ.) — She can speak Chinese now.

Time words like 明天 (míngtiān - tomorrow), 今天 (jīntiān - today), 现在 (xiànzài - now) appear before the modal verb.

## [phase:complex] Choosing the appropriate modal verb

tags: modal-verbs, context, application

Selecting the correct modal verb depends on what you want to express:

**Use 会 when:** talking about skills, learned abilities, or knowledge
- 你会游泳吗？— Can you swim (do you know how)?

**Use 可以 when:** asking permission, granting permission, or stating what's allowed
- 我可以用你的电话吗？— May I use your phone?

**Use 要 when:** expressing intention, desire, advice, or necessity
- 我要学汉语。— I want to learn Chinese.
- 你要多休息。— You should rest more.

## [phase:complex] Modal verbs with location phrases

tags: modal-verbs, location, zai

Modal verbs can combine with location phrases using **在 (zài)** to specify where an action takes place. The location phrase typically follows the modal verb.

**Structure:** Subject + Modal Verb + 在 + Location + Verb

**Examples:**
- 你可以在这里吃饭。(Nǐ kěyǐ zài zhèlǐ chī fàn.) — You can eat here.
- 不可以在这里喝水。(Bù kěyǐ zài zhèlǐ hē shuǐ.) — You can't drink water here.
- 我要在学校学习。(Wǒ yào zài xuéxiào xuéxí.) — I'm going to study at school.

## [phase:complex] Responding to modal verb statements

tags: modal-verbs, responses, conversation

When responding to statements with modal verbs, you can:

**Agree:** Use 对 (duì - correct), 是的 (shì de - yes), or repeat the modal verb
**Disagree:** Use 不 (bù - no) + the modal verb, or offer a correction

**Examples:**
- A: 她会说英语。(Tā huì shuō Yīngyǔ.) — She can speak English.
- B: 对，她会说。(Duì, tā huì shuō.) — Right, she can.

- A: 我可以走了吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ zǒu le ma?) — May I go now?
- B: 可以。(Kěyǐ.) — Yes, you may.

## [phase:integration] Common errors with modal verbs

tags: modal-verbs, errors, correction

**Common mistake 1:** Using 会 for permission
- ❌ 我会进来吗？
- ✓ 我可以进来吗？(May I come in?)

**Common mistake 2:** Using 可以 for learned abilities
- ❌ 我可以说汉语。(sounds like you're asking if it's allowed)
- ✓ 我会说汉语。(I can speak Chinese - learned skill)

**Common mistake 3:** Wrong word order with negation
- ❌ 我会不说英语。
- ✓ 我不会说英语。

**Common mistake 4:** Confusing 不要 (don't want) with 不用 (don't need to)
- 我不要去。— I don't want to go.
- 我不用去。— I don't need to go.

## [phase:integration] Practical scenarios

tags: modal-verbs, scenarios, application

**Scenario 1: New skill**
When you've just learned something new, use 会:
- 我现在会做饭了！(Wǒ xiànzài huì zuò fàn le!) — I can cook now!

**Scenario 2: Asking permission**
Before doing something in someone else's space, use 可以:
- 我可以坐这里吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ zuò zhèlǐ ma?) — May I sit here?

**Scenario 3: Stating intentions**
When talking about plans or desires, use 要:
- 明天我要去买东西。(Míngtiān wǒ yào qù mǎi dōngxi.) — Tomorrow I'm going to buy things.

**Scenario 4: Giving advice**
When suggesting what someone should do, use 要:
- 你要多喝水。(Nǐ yào duō hē shuǐ.) — You should drink more water.

## [phase:integration] Context-dependent meanings

tags: modal-verbs, context, nuance

The same modal verb can have different implications depending on context:

**会 in different contexts:**
- 我会说汉语。— I can speak Chinese. (ability - primary HSK1 meaning)
- 我会来。— I will come. (future - based on knowledge/certainty; secondary usage, more advanced)
- 会下雨。— It will rain. (prediction based on knowledge; secondary usage, more advanced)

**可以 in different contexts:**
- 可以。— Sure. / Okay. (granting permission)
- 你可以走了。— You may go now. (permission)
- 这里可以游泳。— Swimming is allowed here. (possibility/permission)

**要 in different contexts:**
- 我要水。— I want water. (desire)
- 你要小心。— You should be careful. (advice)
- 要下雨了。— It's going to rain. (imminent future; note: the sentence-final 了 indicates imminent change of state, covered in detail in the 了 subtopic)
