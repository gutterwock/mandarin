## [phase:atomic] What is a measure word?
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, introduction

In Chinese, you cannot directly put a number before a noun. You need a **measure word** (量词 liàngcí) between them.

**English:** three books  
**Chinese:** 三**本**书 (sān **běn** shū)

The measure word (本 běn in this example) comes between the number and the noun. Different nouns use different measure words, similar to how English uses "three sheets of paper" or "two cups of coffee."

Without the measure word, the phrase is incomplete and incorrect.

## [phase:atomic] The general measure word 个 (gè)
tags: phase:atomic, measure-word, 个

**个 (gè)** is the most common measure word in Chinese. It is used for people and many general objects.

**一个人** (yī gè rén) — one person  
**两个学生** (liǎng gè xuésheng) — two students  
**三个苹果** (sān gè píngguǒ) — three apples

When you're unsure which measure word to use, 个 (gè) is often acceptable as a default, especially at the beginner level.

## [phase:atomic] The measure word 本 (běn) for books
tags: phase:atomic, measure-word, 本

**本 (běn)** is the measure word specifically for books, magazines, and similar bound publications.

**一本书** (yī běn shū) — one book  
**两本书** (liǎng běn shū) — two books  
**五本书** (wǔ běn shū) — five books

Note: You cannot say ~~一书~~ or ~~两书~~. The measure word 本 is required.

## [phase:atomic] The measure word 杯 (bēi) for cups/glasses
tags: phase:atomic, measure-word, 杯

**杯 (bēi)** is the measure word for beverages in cups or glasses.

**一杯水** (yī bēi shuǐ) — one cup/glass of water  
**两杯茶** (liǎng bēi chá) — two cups of tea  
**三杯咖啡** (sān bēi kāfēi) — three cups of coffee

The word 杯 itself means "cup" or "glass," and it functions as a measure word to quantify the beverage—一杯水 means "one cup-measure-of water" as an integrated counting structure, not "one cup water" as separate concepts.

## [phase:atomic] The measure word 张 (zhāng) for flat objects

## [phase:atomic] The number 两 (liǎng) vs 二 (èr) for "two"
tags: phase:atomic, number, 两-二

Chinese has two words for "two": **二 (èr)** and **两 (liǎng)**.

Before measure words, use **两 (liǎng)**:
- **两个人** (liǎng gè rén) — two people
- **两本书** (liǎng běn shū) — two books
- **两杯水** (liǎng bēi shuǐ) — two cups of water

Use 二 (èr) for counting: 一、二、三、四 (yī, èr, sān, sì).

## [phase:atomic] Building the basic structure
tags: phase:atomic, structure, practice

The complete structure is: **Number + Measure Word + Noun**

**一个人** (yī gè rén) — 1 + [measure word] + person  
**三本书** (sān běn shū) — 3 + [measure word] + book  
**两杯水** (liǎng bēi shuǐ) — 2 + [measure word] + water

Each part is essential. Removing the measure word makes the phrase grammatically wrong.

## [phase:atomic] Common mistake: omitting the measure word
tags: phase:atomic, error, correction

At HSK 1 level, omitting the measure word is a major grammatical error.

**Incorrect:** ~~三人~~ (sān rén)  
**Correct:** 三个人 (sān gè rén)

**Incorrect:** ~~一书~~ (yī shū)  
**Correct:** 一本书 (yī běn shū)

**At HSK 1 level, incomplete phrases like "四杯" are typically considered errors:** Without the noun (water, tea, etc.), the phrase is incomplete for learners. While 四杯 may be grammatically acceptable in advanced contexts where the beverage type is understood from conversation, beginners should always include the full structure: 四杯水 (sì bēi shuǐ) — "four cups of water"

Always include all three parts: number, measure word, and noun.

## [phase:atomic] When to use 个 (gè) as a default
tags: phase:atomic, measure-word, strategy

While different nouns have specific measure words, **个 (gè)** is the safest choice when you're uncertain.

**Specific measure word:** 一本书 (yī běn shū) — one book  
**Default measure word:** 一个苹果 (yī gè píngguǒ) — one apple

Native speakers will understand you even if you use 个 (gè) instead of a more specific measure word, though using the correct specific measure word is better.

**Strategy for HSK 1:** Learn the specific measure words introduced in your course (本, 杯, 张), but use 个 when unsure about others.

## [phase:complex] Choosing the correct measure word
tags: phase:complex, measure-word, application

To choose the right measure word, consider the noun's category:

**People and general objects → 个 (gè)**  
一个人 (yī gè rén), 两个学生 (liǎng gè xuésheng), 三个苹果 (sān gè píngguǒ)

**Books and publications → 本 (běn)**  
一本书 (yī běn shū), 两本杂志 (liǎng běn zázhì)

**Beverages in containers → 杯 (bēi)**  
一杯水 (yī bēi shuǐ), 两杯茶 (liǎng bēi chá), 三杯咖啡 (sān bēi kāfēi)

**Flat objects → 张 (zhāng)**  
一张纸 (yī zhāng zhǐ), 两张桌子 (liǎng zhāng zhuōzi), 三张床 (sān zhāng chuáng)

## [phase:complex] Multiple objects with different measure words

The word 和 (hé) means "and" for connecting items.

## [phase:complex] Distinguishing 两 (liǎng) and 二 (èr)
tags: phase:complex, number, distinction

**Use 两 (liǎng)** before measure words:
- 两个人 (liǎng gè rén) — two people
- 两本书 (liǎng běn shū) — two books
- 两杯水 (liǎng bēi shuǐ) — two cups of water

**Use 二 (èr)** when counting or in compound numbers like 12, 22, etc.:
- 一、二、三 (yī, èr, sān) — one, two, three
- 十二 (shí èr) — twelve
- 二 is also used in ordinal positions (第二 "second") and fractions (二分之一 "one half"), though these are beyond HSK 1 scope

**Never say:** ~~二个人~~, ~~二本书~~, ~~二杯水~~
