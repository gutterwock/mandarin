## [phase:atomic] Recognising Characters in Simple Signs

tags: reading, hanzi-recognition, signs

HSK 1 texts often appear on signs, notices, and simple labels. You should be able to recognise common characters like 人 (person), 出口 (exit), 入口 (entrance), 不 (not), 是 (is), 有 (have), and 在 (at/in).

When reading, identify each character first, then combine them to understand the meaning. For example: 不在 means "not here/absent" by combining 不 (not) + 在 (at/in).

## [phase:atomic] Reading Time Expressions

tags: reading, time, numbers

Time expressions in HSK 1 use numbers with time words. Common patterns include:
- 几点 (what time)
- X点 (X o'clock)
- 今天 (today)
- 明天 (tomorrow)
- 昨天 (yesterday)

Example: 今天三点 means "today at 3 o'clock" (今天 + 三 + 点).

## [phase:atomic] Reading Descriptions with 很

tags: reading, adjectives, modifiers

Adjectives in HSK 1 often appear with 很 (very):
- 很大 (very big)
- 很小 (very small)
- 很好 (very good)
- 很多 (very many/much)
- 很少 (very few/little)

很 links the subject to the adjective: 他很好 (he is very good/fine).

Note: In predicate adjective constructions, 很 is grammatically required and does not always carry the strong meaning of "very" — it functions as a grammatical requirement rather than just an intensifier. For example, 他很好 means "he is fine/good" where 很 is necessary for grammatical correctness, not necessarily emphasizing "very."
