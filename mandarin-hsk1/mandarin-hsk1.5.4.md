# Everyday Objects and Places

## [phase:atomic] 书 (shū) – book
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, objects, noun

The word **书** (shū) means "book." It is one of the most common nouns in Mandarin.

书 is measured with the counter **本** (běn), so "one book" is **一本书** (yì běn shū).

Example sentences:
- 这是我的书。(Zhè shì wǒ de shū.) – This is my book.
- 他有三本书。(Tā yǒu sān běn shū.) – He has three books.

tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, objects, noun, technology

The word **电脑** (diànnǎo) means "computer." Literally, it combines **电** (diàn, "electricity") and **脑** (nǎo, "brain").

## [phase:atomic] 手机 (shǒujī) – mobile phone
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, objects, noun, technology

The word **手机** (shǒujī) means "mobile phone" or "cell phone." It combines **手** (shǒu, "hand") and **机** (jī, "machine").

手机 is measured with the counter **部** (bù), so "one phone" is **一部手机** (yí bù shǒujī). In HSK 1 contexts, **个** (gè) is also commonly used and acceptable, though 部 is more formal.

Example sentences:
- 我的手机在哪儿？(Wǒ de shǒujī zài nǎr?) – Where is my phone?
- 那是他的手机。(Nà shì tā de shǒujī.) – That is his phone.

## [phase:atomic] 水 (shuǐ) – water
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, drinks, noun

The word **水** (shuǐ) means "water." It is an essential everyday vocabulary item.

When counting glasses or bottles of water, use **杯** (bēi, "cup/glass") or **瓶** (píng, "bottle"): **一杯水** (yì bēi shuǐ, "one glass of water").

Example sentences:

## [phase:complex] Object possession and location patterns
tags: phase:complex, grammar, possession, location

You can combine object nouns with possession (有), location (在), and demonstrative patterns:

**Possession pattern:**
- 我有一本书。(Wǒ yǒu yì běn shū.) – I have a book.
- 他有手机吗？(Tā yǒu shǒujī ma?) – Does he have a phone?

**Location pattern:**
- 书在桌子上。(Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.) – The book is on the table.
- 我的手机在哪儿？(Wǒ de shǒujī zài nǎr?) – Where is my phone?

**Demonstrative pattern:**
- 这是我的电脑。(Zhè shì wǒ de diànnǎo.) – This is my computer.
- 那是他的椅子。(Nà shì tā de yǐzi.) – That is his chair.

## [phase:complex] Drinks and meals vocabulary in context
tags: phase:complex, vocabulary, food-drinks, context

- 两杯茶 (liǎng bēi chá) – two cups of tea
- 一碗饭 (yì wǎn fàn) – one bowl of rice

## [phase:complex] Places and directional contexts

## [phase:integration] Describing a room scene
tags: phase:integration, speaking, description

Combining object vocabulary with location words allows you to describe room scenes:

**桌子上有一台电脑和三本书。椅子在桌子前面。我的手机在哪儿？在椅子上。**

(Zhuōzi shàng yǒu yì tái diànnǎo hé sān běn shū. Yǐzi zài zhuōzi qiánmian. Wǒ de shǒujī zài nǎr? Zài yǐzi shàng.)

Translation: "There is a computer and three books on the table. The chair is in front of the table. Where is my phone? It's on the chair."

This combines:
- 有 existence structure
- Object nouns with measure words
- Location phrases (在...上)
- Question-answer format

## [phase:integration] Daily routine and places
tags: phase:integration, speaking, daily-life

You can describe daily routines using place vocabulary:

**我早上在家喝茶。上午我去学校。中午我在饭馆吃饭。下午我去超市。晚上我在家看书。**

(Wǒ zǎoshang zài jiā hē chá. Shàngwǔ wǒ qù xuéxiào. Zhōngwǔ wǒ zài fànguǎn chī fàn. Xiàwǔ wǒ qù chāoshì. Wǎnshang wǒ zài jiā kàn shū.)

Translation: "In the early morning I drink tea at home. In the late morning/forenoon I go to school. At noon I eat at a restaurant. In the afternoon I go to the supermarket. In the evening I read at home."

This integrates:
- Time expressions
- Place nouns
- Action verbs (喝, 去, 吃, 看)
- 在 (location) vs. 去 (direction)

## [phase:integration] Shopping and services scenario
tags: phase:integration, dialogue, practical

This scenario integrates objects and places in a practical context:

**A: 你去哪儿？**
**B: 我去超市。我想买一瓶水和一杯茶。你呢？**
**A: 我去商店。我想买一本书。**
**B: 你的手机在哪儿？**
**A: 在家里的桌子上。**

Translation:
A: Where are you going?
B: I'm going to the supermarket. I want to buy a bottle of water and a cup of tea. How about you?
A: I'm going to the store. I want to buy a book.
B: Where is your phone?
A: On the table at home.

## [phase:integration] Object-location complex descriptions
tags: phase:integration, description, advanced

Advanced descriptions combine multiple objects with precise location information:

**我的家里有很多东西。客厅里有一张桌子和四把椅子。桌子上有一台电脑、两本书和一杯水。我的手机在椅子上。**

(Wǒ de jiā lǐ yǒu hěn duō dōngxi. Kètīng lǐ yǒu yì zhāng zhuōzi hé sì bǎ yǐzi. Zhuōzi shàng yǒu yì tái diànnǎo, liǎng běn shū hé yì bēi shuǐ. Wǒ de shǒujī zài yǐzi shàng.)

Translation: "There are many things in my home. In the living room there is a table and four chairs. On the table there is a computer, two books, and a glass of water. My phone is on the chair."

## [phase:integration] Comprehensive place-based narrative
tags: phase:integration, narrative, synthesis

A complete narrative integrating all place vocabulary:

**NOTE: This narrative includes vocabulary beyond HSK 1 level (marked with †):**

**今天我很忙†。早上我在家喝茶、吃饭。然后†我去学校学习。中午我和朋友†去饭馆吃饭。下午我去超市买水和茶。我也†去商店买书。晚上我不舒服†，去医院看医生†。现在我在家，很累†。**

(Jīntiān wǒ hěn máng. Zǎoshang wǒ zài jiā hē chá, chī fàn. Ránhòu wǒ qù xuéxiào xuéxí. Zhōngwǔ wǒ hé péngyou qù fànguǎn chī fàn. Xiàwǔ wǒ qù chāoshì mǎi shuǐ hé chá. Wǒ yě qù shāngdiàn mǎi shū. Wǎnshang wǒ bù shūfu, qù yīyuàn kàn yīshēng. Xiànzài wǒ zài jiā, hěn lèi.)

Translation: "Today I'm very busy. In the morning I drink tea and eat at home. Then I go to school to study. At noon I go to a restaurant with friends to eat. In the afternoon I go to the supermarket to buy water and tea. I also go to the store to buy books. In the evening I don't feel well, so I go to the hospital to see a doctor. Now I'm at home, very tired."

† = beyond HSK 1 scope; included as extension material for enrichment
