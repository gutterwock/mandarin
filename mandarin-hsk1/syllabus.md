# Mandarin Chinese HSK 1 — Absolute Beginner
id: mandarin-hsk1
description: The four tones, pinyin romanisation, introduction to hanzi, greetings, numbers, basic sentence patterns, core vocabulary to ~300 words. Covers HSK 1.
prerequisites:
- No prior Mandarin or Chinese knowledge required

## Tones, Pinyin, and the Sound System
id: mandarin-hsk1.1
description: The phonological foundations of Mandarin — without tones and pinyin, no vocabulary can be produced correctly.

### The Four Tones
id: mandarin-hsk1.1.1
objectives:
- Produce and distinguish the four tones: 1st (high level ¯), 2nd (rising ˊ), 3rd (dipping ˇ), 4th (falling ˋ)
- Produce the neutral (fifth) tone on unstressed syllables
- Identify each tone by its pitch contour in isolation and in simple words

### Tone Sandhi and Tone Rules
id: mandarin-hsk1.1.2
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.1.1
objectives:
- Apply third-tone sandhi: 3rd + 3rd → 2nd + 3rd (你好 nǐhǎo → níhǎo)
- Apply 不 (bù) tone change: 4th before 4th → 2nd before 4th (不是 bú shì)
- Apply 一 (yī) tone changes before 1st/2nd/3rd → 4th; before 4th → 2nd

### Pinyin: Initials and Finals
id: mandarin-hsk1.1.3
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.1.1
objectives:
- Produce the 21 initials (声母), including sounds without English equivalents: x, q, zh, ch, sh, r
- Produce the main finals (韵母): simple (a, o, e, i, u, ü), compound (ai, ei, ao, ou...), nasal (an, en, ang, eng, ong...)
- Read pinyin syllables by combining initials + finals + tone marks

### Pinyin: Spelling Rules and Special Cases
id: mandarin-hsk1.1.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.1.3
objectives:
- Apply the i/u/ü zero-initial spelling rules: i→yi, u→wu, ü→yu
- Apply tone mark placement rules (mark goes on the main vowel)
- Recognise erhua (儿化) notation and its effect on pronunciation

### Introduction to Hanzi
id: mandarin-hsk1.1.5
objectives:
- Explain the difference between simplified and traditional characters
- Identify the role of radicals (部首) as meaning clues in hanzi
- Write the basic strokes in correct order: 横, 竖, 撇, 捺, 点, 折
- Read pinyin alongside hanzi as a learning aid throughout HSK 1

## Greetings and Basic Social Language
id: mandarin-hsk1.2
description: Formulaic social exchanges — the first language a learner needs before any grammar.

### Core Greetings
id: mandarin-hsk1.2.1
objectives:
- Use: 你好 (hello), 您好 (formal hello), 早上好 (good morning), 晚上好 (good evening), 再见 (goodbye), 晚安 (good night)
- Understand when 您好 is preferred over 你好 (formality/respect)
- Respond appropriately to each greeting

### Thanks, Apologies, and Courtesy
id: mandarin-hsk1.2.2
objectives:
- Use: 谢谢 (thank you), 不客气 / 没关系 (you're welcome / no problem), 对不起 (I'm sorry), 没关系 (it's okay)
- Distinguish 对不起 (apology) from 请 (please/inviting action)
- Use 请 in service contexts: 请进 (please come in), 请坐 (please sit)

### Introductions and Names
id: mandarin-hsk1.2.3
objectives:
- Introduce yourself: 我叫 [name] (my name is), 我是 [nationality/role] (I am)
- Ask someone's name: 你叫什么名字？ / 你是哪国人？
- Respond to introductions: 认识你很高兴 (nice to meet you)

### How Are You and Simple Responses
id: mandarin-hsk1.2.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.2.1
objectives:
- Ask and respond to: 你好吗？我很好，谢谢。你呢？
- Use 还好 (so-so / not bad), 不太好 (not very well), 很忙 (very busy)
- Understand that 你好吗？is less common in daily speech than in textbooks; prefer 最近怎么样？at higher levels

## Numbers, Time, and Dates
id: mandarin-hsk1.3
description: Numbers and time expressions needed for everyday transactions and scheduling.

### Numbers 0–100
id: mandarin-hsk1.3.1
objectives:
- Count from 0 (零) to 100: 一, 二, 三 ... 十, 十一 ... 二十, 二十一 ... 百
- Distinguish 两 (liǎng) from 二 (èr): 两 is used before measure words and in some compounds
- Read and write prices and quantities in hanzi and Arabic numerals

### Large Numbers and Money
id: mandarin-hsk1.3.2
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.3.1
objectives:
- Form numbers from 100 to 10,000 (百, 千, 万)
- Read prices in Chinese: 元 / 块 (yuan), 角 / 毛 (jiǎo), 分 (fēn)
- Ask and understand prices: 多少钱？ / 这个多少钱？

### Telling the Time
id: mandarin-hsk1.3.3
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.3.1
objectives:
- Tell the time: 几点了？ → X点 (Y分) / X点半 (half past)
- Use 上午 (AM) and 下午 (PM); 中午 (noon) and 晚上 (evening)
- Ask and answer: 现在几点？ / 你几点起床？

### Days, Months, and Dates
id: mandarin-hsk1.3.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.3.1
objectives:
- Name the days of the week: 星期一 through 星期日 / 星期天
- Form dates: X年X月X号 (year/month/day)
- Use time words: 今天, 明天, 昨天, 这个星期, 下个月, 去年, 今年, 明年

### Time Expressions and Position
id: mandarin-hsk1.3.5
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.3.4
objectives:
- Use time words in sentence-initial or pre-verb position (not end): 我明天去。
- Produce daily routine time expressions: 早上, 上午, 中午, 下午, 晚上, 每天
- Sequence activities using 先...再... (first...then...) and 然后 (then/after that)

## Basic Sentence Patterns
id: mandarin-hsk1.4
description: The core grammatical frames of Mandarin — SVO word order, the copula 是, existence 有, location 在, and negation.

### SVO Word Order
id: mandarin-hsk1.4.1
objectives:
- Produce basic Subject + Verb + Object sentences: 我喝茶。/ 她学汉语。
- Understand that Mandarin is strictly SVO; adjectives and adverbs precede what they modify
- Position time and place expressions before the verb (not at the end as in English)

### 是 (shì): The Copula
id: mandarin-hsk1.4.2
objectives:
- Form X是Y sentences: 我是学生。/ 这是书。
- Negate with 不是: 他不是老师。
- Form questions with 吗: 你是中国人吗？and answer 是 / 不是

### 有 (yǒu): Possession and Existence
id: mandarin-hsk1.4.3
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.4.2
objectives:
- Use 有 for possession: 我有一本书 (I have a book)
- Use 有 for existence: 桌子上有一杯水 (There is a glass of water on the table)
- Negate with 没有 (not 不有): 我没有车

### 在 (zài): Location
id: mandarin-hsk1.4.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.4.2
objectives:
- Use 在 to indicate where something/someone is: 我在家。/ 书在桌子上。
- Use 在 before place words: 在学校, 在北京, 在哪里？
- Distinguish 在 (location of existence) from 有 (existence of an item at a location)

### Negation: 不 and 没
id: mandarin-hsk1.4.5
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.4.3
objectives:
- Use 不 to negate verbs (present/future) and adjectives: 我不去。/ 不贵。
- Use 没 (méi) to negate 有 and to negate completed actions with 了
- Recognise that 不好 (not good) and 没有 (don't have / haven't) are the two dominant negation patterns

## Measure Words and Nouns
id: mandarin-hsk1.5
description: Mandarin requires a measure word between every number and noun — this topic covers the most essential ones.

### The Structure: Number + Measure Word + Noun
id: mandarin-hsk1.5.1
objectives:
- Produce the Number + Measure Word + Noun frame: 一个人, 三本书, 两杯水
- Use 个 (gè) as the default/general measure word when unsure
- Understand that omitting the measure word is a major error at HSK 1

### Core Measure Words
id: mandarin-hsk1.5.2
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.5.1
objectives:
- Use the most common HSK 1 measure words with their nouns: 个 (people, general), 本 (books), 杯 (cups/glasses), 张 (flat things), 块 (money/pieces), 只 (small animals), 条 (long thin things), 件 (clothing/matters)
- Ask "how many" with 几 (small numbers) and 多少 (larger quantities)

### People and Relationships
id: mandarin-hsk1.5.3
objectives:
- Identify and use ~20 HSK 1 relationship and people words: 我, 你, 他, 她, 我们, 你们, 他们, 爸爸, 妈妈, 哥哥, 姐姐, 弟弟, 妹妹, 朋友, 老师, 同学, 医生
- Form family descriptions: 我有一个哥哥和一个妹妹
- Use 的 to form possessives: 我的妈妈, 他的朋友

### Everyday Objects and Places
id: mandarin-hsk1.5.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.5.3
objectives:
- Identify and use ~20 HSK 1 object and place words: 书, 电脑, 手机, 水, 茶, 饭, 椅子, 桌子, 家, 学校, 医院, 商店, 饭馆, 超市
- Form sentences combining objects with possession, location, and existence patterns

## Core Verbs and Actions
id: mandarin-hsk1.6
description: The most frequent action verbs at HSK 1 — movement, activity, want, and modal verbs.

### Movement Verbs
id: mandarin-hsk1.6.1
objectives:
- Use core movement verbs: 来 (come), 去 (go), 回 (return), 进 (enter), 出 (exit), 上 (go up), 下 (go down)
- Attach destination with 去/来 + place: 去学校, 来我家
- Use directional complements: 上来, 下去, 进来, 出去 (introduction only)

### Activity Verbs
id: mandarin-hsk1.6.2
objectives:
- Use core activity verbs: 吃 (eat), 喝 (drink), 看 (watch/read), 听 (listen), 说 (speak), 写 (write), 读 (read aloud), 买 (buy), 开 (open/drive), 做 (do/make), 睡觉 (sleep), 工作 (work), 学习 (study)
- Form simple SVO sentences with each verb
- Pair verbs with their most common objects: 吃饭, 喝水, 看书, 听音乐

### 喜欢 and 想: Likes and Wants
id: mandarin-hsk1.6.3
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.6.2
objectives:
- Use 喜欢 + noun/verb to express likes: 我喜欢中国菜。/ 我喜欢看电影。
- Use 想 + verb to express desire: 我想喝茶。/ 她想去北京。
- Negate with 不喜欢 and 不想; distinguish from 不要 (don't want to / don't)

### Modal Verbs: 会, 可以, 要
id: mandarin-hsk1.6.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.6.3
objectives:
- Use 会 for learned ability: 我会说汉语。/ 她会开车。
- Use 可以 for permission: 我可以进来吗？/ 不可以在这里吃东西。
- Use 要 for intention or necessity: 我要去学校。/ 你要多喝水。

### Aspect Marker 了: Completion
id: mandarin-hsk1.6.5
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.6.2
objectives:
- Use sentence-final 了 to indicate a change of state or completed action: 我吃了。/ 他来了。
- Distinguish from verb-suffix 了 (completed action): 我吃了饭 (I ate/have eaten rice)
- Negate completed actions with 没有: 我没有吃饭

## Questions and Interrogatives
id: mandarin-hsk1.7
description: All major question types in Mandarin — yes/no questions, A-not-A, question words, and 呢.

### Yes/No Questions with 吗
id: mandarin-hsk1.7.1
objectives:
- Form questions by adding 吗 to statements: 你是老师吗？/ 你有手机吗？
- Answer with the verb (not yes/no): 是 / 不是; 有 / 没有; 去 / 不去
- Understand that 吗 takes rising intonation and statement word order is retained

### A-Not-A Questions
id: mandarin-hsk1.7.2
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.7.1
objectives:
- Form V-not-V questions: 你去不去？/ 你是不是学生？/ 好不好？
- Understand A-not-A as a neutral alternative to 吗 questions (no added politeness difference)
- Use 有没有 as the A-not-A form of 有: 你有没有时间？

### Question Words
id: mandarin-hsk1.7.3
objectives:
- Use the HSK 1 question words in-sentence (not sentence-final): 什么 (what), 谁 (who), 哪 (which), 哪里/哪儿 (where), 什么时候 (when), 为什么 (why), 怎么 (how), 多少 (how many/much), 几 (how many, small numbers)
- Recognise that question words stay in normal sentence position (no fronting): 你叫什么名字？

### 呢: Returning a Question and Topic Continuity
id: mandarin-hsk1.7.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.7.3
objectives:
- Use 呢 to return a question: 我很好，你呢？
- Use 呢 to ask "what about X?": 那本书呢？
- Understand 呢 as a soft question particle implying the topic is already established

## Descriptions and Adjectives
id: mandarin-hsk1.8
description: Stative verbs as predicates, degree adverbs, colours, and basic appearance vocabulary.

### Stative Verbs as Predicate Adjectives
id: mandarin-hsk1.8.1
objectives:
- Use adjectives directly as predicates with 很 (required as a dummy adverb when no other adverb is present): 他很高。/ 这个苹果很好吃。
- Negate adjectives with 不: 不贵, 不好看, 不冷
- Understand that bare adjective predicates (without 很) imply comparison: 他高 = he is taller (than expected/someone else)

### Degree Adverbs
id: mandarin-hsk1.8.2
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.8.1
objectives:
- Use 很 (very), 太 (too/extremely), 非常 (extremely), 真 (really), 有点儿 (a bit, slightly negative), 比较 (relatively/rather), 不太 (not very)
- Distinguish 太...了 (too..., often negative) from 非常 (neutral intensity)
- Use 有点儿 only with adjectives that imply a problem or undesirability

### Colours and Basic Appearance
id: mandarin-hsk1.8.3
objectives:
- Use core colour words: 红 (red), 白 (white), 黑 (black), 黄 (yellow), 绿 (green), 蓝 (blue), 粉 (pink)
- Use basic appearance adjectives: 大 (big), 小 (small), 高 (tall), 矮 (short), 长 (long), 短 (short), 胖 (fat), 瘦 (thin), 漂亮 (pretty), 帅 (handsome)
- Form attributive adjective + 的 + noun phrases: 红色的衣服, 高的男人

### Expressing Preferences and Opinions
id: mandarin-hsk1.8.4
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.8.2
objectives:
- Express simple preferences: 我觉得...很好 (I think... is good), 我认为...不错 (I think... is not bad)
- Use 好吃, 好看, 好听 (delicious, good-looking, good-sounding) and their negatives
- Ask for opinions: 你觉得怎么样？ / 你喜欢吗？

id: mandarin-hsk1.10.3
prerequisites:
- mandarin-hsk1.1.5
objectives:
- Read short sentences and notices written in HSK 1 hanzi

- Recognise all HSK 1 characters in print form
