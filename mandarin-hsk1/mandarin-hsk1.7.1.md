## [phase:atomic] Forming Questions with 吗 (ma)
tags: question-formation, particle, sentence-structure

In Mandarin Chinese, one of the simplest ways to form a yes/no question is by adding the particle **吗 (ma)** to the end of a statement. The word order remains exactly the same as the statement — you simply add 吗 at the end and use rising intonation.

**Pattern:** Statement + 吗？

Examples:
- Statement: **你是学生。** (Nǐ shì xuésheng.) — You are a student.
- Question: **你是学生吗？** (Nǐ shì xuésheng ma?) — Are you a student?

The 吗 particle doesn't have a meaning by itself; it only serves to turn statements into questions.

## [phase:atomic] Word Order Stays the Same
tags: word-order, sentence-structure

When you add 吗 to create a question, the word order of the original statement does **not** change. Unlike English, where we often invert the subject and verb ("You are" becomes "Are you?"), Chinese keeps the exact same word order.

**Statement word order:** Subject + Verb + Object
**Question word order:** Subject + Verb + Object + 吗？

Examples:
- **他喝茶。** (Tā hē chá.) — He drinks tea.
- **他喝茶吗？** (Tā hē chá ma?) — Does he drink tea?

## [phase:atomic] Rising Intonation with 吗
tags: pronunciation, intonation

When you say a sentence ending with 吗, use **rising intonation** at the end, similar to how English speakers raise their pitch at the end of yes/no questions. This rising tone reinforces that you're asking a question, not making a statement.

Even though 吗 has a neutral tone (indicated by no tone mark), the overall sentence melody rises at the end to signal a question.

## [phase:atomic] Answering with the Verb
tags: answering-questions, verb-response

In Mandarin, you **do not** answer yes/no questions with words for "yes" or "no" (there is no direct equivalent of "yes" in Mandarin). Instead, you answer by **repeating the main verb** from the question.

- To affirm: repeat the verb
- To negate: add 不 (bù) before the verb

Example:
- Question: **你是学生吗？** (Are you a student?)
- Affirmative: **是。** (Shì.) — [I] am.
- Negative: **不是。** (Bù shì.) — [I] am not.

## [phase:atomic] Negative Answers with 不
tags: negation, answering-questions

To give a negative answer to a 吗 question, use **不 (bù)** + the main verb. The 不 particle negates the verb.

Examples:
- Question: **你去吗？** (Nǐ qù ma?) — Are you going?
- Negative answer: **不去。** (Bù qù.) — [I'm] not going.

- Question: **你是老师吗？** (Nǐ shì lǎoshī ma?) — Are you a teacher?
- Negative answer: **不是。** (Bù shì.) — [I'm] not.

## [phase:atomic] Special Case: 有 (yǒu) Uses 没有 for Negation
tags: negation, special-cases

The verb **有 (yǒu)** meaning "to have" is an exception to the standard negation pattern. While most verbs use 不 for negation, 有 uses **没有 (méiyǒu)** or simply **没 (méi)**.

Examples:
- Question: **你有手机吗？** (Nǐ yǒu shǒujī ma?) — Do you have a phone?
- Affirmative: **有。** (Yǒu.) — [I] have [one].
- Negative: **没有。** (Méiyǒu.) or **没。** (Méi.) — [I] don't have [one].

**Note:** Using 不有 is incorrect.

## [phase:atomic] Basic 吗 Questions with 是 (shì)
tags: verb-shi, identity-questions

The verb **是 (shì)** means "to be" and is commonly used with 吗 questions to ask about identity, nationality, occupation, or classification.

Pattern: Subject + 是 + Noun + 吗？

Examples:
- **你是中国人吗？** (Nǐ shì Zhōngguórén ma?) — Are you Chinese?
- **他是医生吗？** (Tā shì yīshēng ma?) — Is he a doctor?

## [phase:atomic] Basic 吗 Questions with Action Verbs
tags: action-verbs, verb-usage

You can form 吗 questions with any action verb. Common HSK 1 action verbs include **去 (qù)** "to go," **来 (lái)** "to come," **看 (kàn)** "to watch/look," **喝 (hē)** "to drink," and **吃 (chī)** "to eat."

Examples:
- **你去商店吗？** (Nǐ qù shāngdiàn ma?) — Are you going to the store?
- **他吃米饭吗？** (Tā chī mǐfàn ma?) — Does he eat rice?

## [phase:atomic] Answering 是 Questions
tags: verb-shi, answering-questions

When answering questions formed with **是 (shì)**, respond with:
- Affirmative: **是。** (Shì.)
- Negative: **不是。** (Bù shì.)

You can also add more information after the verb:
- **是，我是学生。** (Shì, wǒ shì xuésheng.) — Yes, I am a student.
- **不是，我是老师。** (Bù shì, wǒ shì lǎoshī.) — No, I am a teacher.

## [phase:complex] Converting Statements to Questions
tags: sentence-transformation, practice

To convert any statement into a 吗 question, follow these steps:
1. Keep the entire statement exactly as is
2. Add 吗 at the very end
3. Change the period to a question mark
4. Use rising intonation when speaking

Practice this with various statement types: identity (是), possession (有), and action verbs.

Examples:
- **她喜欢猫。** → **她喜欢猫吗？** (Does she like cats?)
- **你们学习汉语。** → **你们学习汉语吗？** (Do you study Chinese?)

## [phase:complex] Question and Answer Pairs
tags: conversation, response-patterns

In natural conversation, questions and answers form pairs. The answer must match the verb used in the question. Practice recognizing which verb appears in the question and using it correctly in the response.

If the question uses 是, answer with 是/不是.
If the question uses 有, answer with 有/没有.
If the question uses an action verb like 去, answer with that verb: 去/不去.

## [phase:complex] Multiple 吗 Questions in Context
tags: conversation, context

In real conversations, you'll encounter multiple 吗 questions in sequence. Each question must be answered independently with its own verb. Pay attention to what each question is asking about.

Example exchange:
- A: **你是学生吗？** (Are you a student?)
- B: **是。** (Yes.)
- A: **你学习汉语吗？** (Do you study Chinese?)
- B: **学习。** (Yes, I do.)

## [phase:complex] Common Mistakes with 吗 Questions
tags: error-correction, common-errors

Common errors when forming and answering 吗 questions include:

1. **Changing word order:** ❌ 吗你是学生？ ✓ 你是学生吗？
2. **Using 不有 instead of 没有:** ❌ 不有 ✓ 没有
3. **Answering with English "yes/no":** Answer must use the Chinese verb
4. **Mismatching the verb:** If asked with 去, don't answer with 是

## [phase:integration] Distinguishing Question Types
tags: question-types, comparison

As you advance, you'll learn that Mandarin has multiple question types. The 吗 question is just one type. Understanding when to use 吗 (versus other question forms you'll learn later) is important:

**吗 questions** are used when:
- You expect a yes/no answer
- You're confirming something
- You don't know whether the statement is true

For example: **你喝茶吗？** (Do you drink tea?) — You don't know if they do or not.

## [phase:integration] Complex Answer Patterns
tags: extended-answers, elaboration

While short verb answers are correct, native speakers often provide fuller responses in natural conversation. You can:
- Repeat the verb and add more information
- Use the full sentence structure
- Add reasoning with 因为 (yīnwèi, "because")

Examples:
- Q: **你学习汉语吗？**
- A: **学习，我学习汉语。** (Yes, I study Chinese.)
- A: **学习，因为我喜欢中国。** (Yes, because I like China.)
