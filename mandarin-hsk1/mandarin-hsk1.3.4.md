# Days, Months, and Dates

## [phase:atomic] 星期 (xīngqī) – "Week"
tags: vocabulary, time

The word **星期** (xīngqī) means "week" and is used to form days of the week. It literally means "star period."

When asking "What day is it?" you say: **星期几？** (xīngqī jǐ?) using the question word 几 (jǐ) for "which number."

## [phase:atomic] Days of the Week: Monday to Friday
tags: vocabulary, time

Days of the week follow a numbered pattern using 星期 + number:

- **星期一** (xīngqī yī) – Monday ("week one")
- **星期二** (xīngqī èr) – Tuesday ("week two")
- **星期三** (xīngqī sān) – Wednesday ("week three")
- **星期四** (xīngqī sì) – Thursday ("week four")
- **星期五** (xīngqī wǔ) – Friday ("week five")

Note that Monday is considered day 1, not Sunday.

## [phase:atomic] Days of the Week: Saturday and Sunday
tags: vocabulary, time

The weekend days are:

- **星期六** (xīngqī liù) – Saturday ("week six")
- **星期日** (xīngqī rì) or **星期天** (xīngqī tiān) – Sunday

Sunday has two common forms: 日 (rì, "sun/day") and 天 (tiān, "day/sky"). Both are equally correct and widely used.

## [phase:atomic] 今天 (jīntiān) – "Today"
tags: vocabulary, time

**今天** (jīntiān) means "today." It combines 今 (jīn, "current/now") with 天 (tiān, "day").

Examples:
- 今天星期几？ (jīntiān xīngqī jǐ?) – What day is today?
- 今天星期一。 (jīntiān xīngqī yī.) – Today is Monday.

## [phase:atomic] 年 (nián) – "Year"
tags: vocabulary, time

**年** (nián) means "year." To form a year, say each digit individually followed by 年.

Examples:
- **2024年** – èr líng èr sì nián (2-0-2-4 year)
- **2019年** – èr líng yī jiǔ nián (2-0-1-9 year)

Note: Use 零 (líng) for "zero" in years.

## [phase:atomic] 号 (hào) – "Day of the Month"
tags: vocabulary, time

**号** (hào) means "day of the month" or "date." Days are formed by number + 号:

- **一号** (yī hào) – the 1st
- **十五号** (shíwǔ hào) – the 15th
- **二十三号** (èrshísān hào) – the 23rd

Example: 今天几号？ (jīntiān jǐ hào?) – What's the date today?

Note: **日** (rì, meaning "sun" or "day") is an alternative formal/written form for day of the month, though 号 (hào) is more common in spoken Chinese. Both may appear in texts and could be tested at HSK1 level.

## [phase:atomic] Date Format: Year-Month-Day
tags: grammar, time

Complete dates in Mandarin follow the pattern: **年 (year) + 月 (month) + 号 (day)**

Format: **X年X月X号**

Examples:
- **2024年3月15号** (èr líng èr sì nián sānyuè shíwǔ hào) – March 15, 2024
- **2023年12月1号** – December 1, 2023

This follows the logical order from largest to smallest time unit.

## [phase:complex] Answering "What Day Is It?"
tags: conversation, time

When someone asks **今天星期几？** (jīntiān xīngqī jǐ? – What day is today?), you respond with:

**今天星期** + [day number]

Example dialogue:
- A: 今天星期几？
- B: 今天星期二。 (jīntiān xīngqī èr.) – Today is Tuesday.

You can also ask about other days:
- 明天星期几？ (míngtiān xīngqī jǐ?) – What day is tomorrow?
- 昨天星期几？ (zuótiān xīngqī jǐ?) – What day was yesterday?

## [phase:complex] Asking About Dates
tags: conversation, time

To ask "What's the date?" you can say:

- **今天几月几号？** (jīntiān jǐ yuè jǐ hào?) – What month and day is today?
- **今天几号？** (jīntiān jǐ hào?) – What day (of the month) is today?

Response format: **今天** + [month] + [day]

Example:
- Q: 今天几月几号？
- A: 今天三月二十五号。 (jīntiān sānyuè èrshíwǔ hào.) – Today is March 25th.

## [phase:complex] Talking About Birth Dates
tags: conversation, time

To ask "When is your birthday?" say:

**你的生日是几月几号？** (nǐ de shēngrì shì jǐ yuè jǐ hào?)

- 你的 (nǐ de) – your
- 生日 (shēngrì) – birthday
- 是 (shì) – is
- 几月几号 (jǐ yuè jǐ hào) – what month what day

Response: **我的生日是** + [month] + [day]

Example: 我的生日是五月十二号。 (wǒ de shēngrì shì wǔyuè shí'èr hào.) – My birthday is May 12th.

## [phase:complex] Using 上 (shàng) for "Last"
tags: vocabulary, time

The word **上** (shàng) means "above" or "last/previous" when used with time words:

- **上个星期** (shàngge xīngqī) – last week
- **上个月** (shàngge yuè) – last month

Compare with 下 (xià, "next"):
- 上个月 (shàngge yuè) – last month
- 这个月 (zhège yuè) – this month  
- 下个月 (xiàge yuè) – next month

## [phase:complex] Complete Date Expressions
tags: grammar, time

Full dates can include the day of the week:

**[Year]年[Month]月[Day]号星期[Day of Week]**

Example: **2024年3月15号星期五** (èr líng èr sì nián sānyuè shíwǔ hào xīngqī wǔ) – Friday, March 15, 2024

However, more commonly you'll see:
- Just the date: 3月15号
- Date + day: 3月15号星期五
- Or in conversation, just the day: 星期五

## [phase:complex] Time Word Patterns
tags: grammar, time

Chinese time words follow consistent patterns:

**Relative days:**
- 昨天 (zuótiān) – yesterday
- 今天 (jīntiān) – today
- 明天 (míngtiān) – tomorrow

**Relative years:**
- 去年 (qùnián) – last year
- 今年 (jīnnián) – this year
- 明年 (míngnián) – next year

**Relative weeks/months (with 个):**
- 上个星期 – last week / 这个星期 – this week / 下个星期 – next week
- 上个月 – last month / 这个月 – this month / 下个月 – next month

Note: Days use special compounds (昨/今/明), while weeks/months use 上个/这个/下个.

## [phase:integration] Planning a Meeting
tags: conversation, application

**Dialogue:**

李明：我们什么时候见面？  
(Lǐ Míng: wǒmen shénme shíhou jiànmiàn?)

王丽：下个星期怎么样？  
(Wáng Lì: xiàge xīngqī zěnmeyàng?)

李明：好。星期几？  
(Lǐ Míng: hǎo. xīngqī jǐ?)

王丽：星期三，五月十号。  
(Wáng Lì: xīngqī sān, wǔyuè shí hào.)

**Vocabulary:**
- 我们 (wǒmen) – we/us
- **什么时候** (shénme shíhou) – when/what time. Used to ask about specific times or scheduling. 什么 (shénme) = "what," 时候 (shíhou) = "time."
- 见面 (jiànmiàn) – meet (face to face)
- 怎么样 (zěnmeyàng) – how about

**Using 什么时候:**
- 你什么时候有时间？ (nǐ shénme shíhou yǒu shíjiān?) – When do you have time?
- 我们什么时候见面？ (wǒmen shénme shíhou jiànmiàn?) – When shall we meet?

## [phase:integration] 可以 (kěyǐ) – "Can/May"
tags: vocabulary, application

**可以** (kěyǐ) means "can," "may," or "is possible." It's commonly used to express availability or willingness:

- **我星期五可以。** (wǒ xīngqī wǔ kěyǐ.) – I can (do it) on Friday.
- **下午可以吗？** (xiàwǔ kěyǐ ma?) – Is afternoon okay?
