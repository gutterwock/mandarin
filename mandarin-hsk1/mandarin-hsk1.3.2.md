# Large Numbers and Money

## [phase:atomic] Numbers 100-999 with 百 (bǎi)
tags: numbers, hundred, place-value

百 (bǎi) means "hundred" in Chinese. To form hundreds, place a digit (1-9) before 百:

- 一百 (yī bǎi) = 100
- 二百 (èr bǎi) = 200
- 三百 (sān bǎi) = 300
- 九百 (jiǔ bǎi) = 900

For numbers like 150, combine 百 with tens and ones: 一百五十 (yī bǎi wǔ shí).

For numbers like 205, use 零 (líng) for the zero placeholder: 二百零五 (èr bǎi líng wǔ).

## [phase:atomic] Zero placeholder 零 in hundreds
tags: numbers, zero, placeholder

When there's a gap in place values (like 101, 205, 408), use 零 (líng) as a placeholder:

- 一百零一 (yī bǎi líng yī) = 101
- 三百零五 (sān bǎi líng wǔ) = 305
- 六百零八 (liù bǎi líng bā) = 608

You don't use 零 when the tens place has a value: 一百五十 (yī bǎi wǔ shí) = 150 (no 零 needed).

## [phase:atomic] Numbers 1,000-9,999 with 千 (qiān)
tags: numbers, thousand, place-value

千 (qiān) means "thousand." Place a digit (1-9) before 千:

- 一千 (yī qiān) = 1,000
- 二千 (èr qiān) = 2,000
- 五千 (wǔ qiān) = 5,000

For numbers like 1,500, combine: 一千五百 (yī qiān wǔ bǎi).

For numbers like 2,050, use 零: 二千零五十 (èr qiān líng wǔ shí).

## [phase:atomic] Zero placeholder in thousands
tags: numbers, zero, thousand

Use 零 (líng) for missing place values in thousands:

- 一千零一 (yī qiān líng yī) = 1,001 (no hundreds, no tens)
- 二千零五十 (èr qiān líng wǔ shí) = 2,050 (no hundreds)
- 三千零八 (sān qiān líng bā) = 3,008 (no hundreds, no tens)

But: 一千五百 (yī qiān wǔ bǎi) = 1,500 (no 零 because hundreds are present).

## [phase:atomic] Ten thousand: 万 (wàn)
tags: numbers, ten-thousand, large-numbers

Chinese counts in units of ten thousand. 万 (wàn) = 10,000:

- 一万 (yī wàn) = 10,000
- 二万 (èr wàn) = 20,000
- 十万 (shí wàn) = 100,000

Unlike English which groups by thousands, Chinese groups by 万 (ten thousands).

## [phase:atomic] Currency: 元 (yuán) and 块 (kuài)
tags: money, currency, yuan

The official Chinese currency unit is 元 (yuán), also called 块 (kuài) in spoken Chinese:

- 五元 (wǔ yuán) = 5 yuan (formal/written)
- 五块 (wǔ kuài) = 5 yuan (spoken/informal)

Both mean the same thing. 块 is more common in daily conversation.

## [phase:atomic] Currency subdivision: 角 (jiǎo) and 毛 (máo)
tags: money, currency, subdivision

角 (jiǎo) is 1/10 of a yuan (10 cents). In spoken Chinese, it's called 毛 (máo):

- 一角 (yī jiǎo) = 0.1 yuan (formal)
- 一毛 (yī máo) = 0.1 yuan (spoken)
- 五角 (wǔ jiǎo) = 0.5 yuan
- 九毛 (jiǔ máo) = 0.9 yuan

## [phase:atomic] Currency subdivision: 分 (fēn)
tags: money, currency, subdivision

分 (fēn) is 1/100 of a yuan (1 cent):

- 一分 (yī fēn) = 0.01 yuan
- 五分 (wǔ fēn) = 0.05 yuan

分 is rarely used today because prices are usually in whole yuan or jiǎo, but it's still part of the currency system.

## [phase:atomic] Asking prices: 这个多少钱 (zhège duōshao qián)
tags: money, questions, demonstrative

这个 (zhège) means "this one" or "this." Combine it with 多少钱？to ask about specific items:

- 这个多少钱？(zhège duōshao qián?) = How much is this?
- 那个多少钱？(nàge duōshao qián?) = How much is that?

这个 points to things near you; 那个 points to things farther away.

## [phase:complex] Shortened price expressions
tags: money, colloquial, shortcuts

In spoken Chinese, prices are often shortened:

- 三块五 (sān kuài wǔ) = 3.5 yuan (毛 omitted)
- 十块八 (shí kuài bā) = 10.8 yuan (毛 omitted)
- 两百五 (liǎng bǎi wǔ) = 250 yuan (十 omitted from 五十)

This is informal but very common in everyday shopping. Note: 两百五 is ambiguous — while it most commonly means 250, it could mean 205 in some contexts. Context usually makes the meaning clear, but be aware of this potential ambiguity.
