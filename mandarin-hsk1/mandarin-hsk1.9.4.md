## [phase:atomic] 天气 (tiānqì) – Weather
tags: vocabulary, weather, noun

The word **天气** (tiānqì) means "weather." It combines 天 (tiān, sky/day) and 气 (qì, air/atmosphere).

Common usage: 今天天气怎么样？(Jīntiān tiānqì zěnmeyàng?) – "How is the weather today?"

## [phase:atomic] Weather Question Pattern: 天气怎么样？
tags: grammar, weather, question-pattern

To ask "How is the weather?" in Chinese, use: **...天气怎么样？** (...tiānqì zěnmeyàng?)

## [phase:complex] Describing Weather Conditions
tags: weather, description, sentence-patterns

Weather descriptions in Chinese typically follow this pattern:

[time] + 天气 + 很 + [adjective]
OR
[time] + [weather verb]

Examples:
- 今天天气很热。(Jīntiān tiānqì hěn rè.) – "Today's weather is very hot."
- 今天很冷。(Jīntiān hěn lěng.) – "Today is very cold."
- 今天下雨。(Jīntiān xià yǔ.) – "It's raining today."

Note that 很 (hěn) is often used before adjectives even when "very" isn't emphasized in English.

## [phase:complex] Talking About Health and Discomfort
tags: health, description, sentence-patterns

To describe feeling unwell in Chinese, common patterns include:

**General discomfort:**
- 我不舒服。(Wǒ bù shūfu.) – "I don't feel well."

**Specific pain:**
- 我 + [body part] + 很疼。
- Example: 我头很疼。(Wǒ tóu hěn téng.) – "My head hurts a lot." / "I have a bad headache."

The structure places the body part directly after the subject, without 的 (de).

## [phase:complex] Medical Contexts: 医院 and 医生
tags: health, vocabulary, context

The words 医院 (yīyuàn, hospital) and 医生 (yīshēng, doctor) are frequently used together when discussing medical situations:

- 我去医院。(Wǒ qù yīyuàn.) – "I'm going to the hospital."
- 他是医生。(Tā shì yīshēng.) – "He is a doctor."
- 我要看医生。(Wǒ yào kàn yīshēng.) – "I want to see a doctor."

The verb 看 (kàn, to see/look at) is used with 医生 to mean "see a doctor." Note: The modal verb 要 (yào, want/need to) was introduced in subtopic 6.4, so this usage is within the curriculum scope.

## [phase:complex] Connecting Weather and Health
tags: weather, health, causation

In Chinese conversation, weather and health are often connected. Cold weather might make you uncomfortable, or hot weather might affect how you feel:

- 今天很冷，我不舒服。(Jīntiān hěn lěng, wǒ bù shūfu.) – "Today is very cold, I don't feel well."
- 天气很热。(Tiānqì hěn rè.) – "The weather is very hot."

The comma (，) or period (。) separates related statements.
