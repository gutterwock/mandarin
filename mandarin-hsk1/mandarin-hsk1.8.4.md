# Expressing Preferences and Opinions

## [phase:atomic] Expressing "I think" with 觉得 (juéde)
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, expressing-opinion

The verb **觉得** (juéde) means "to think" or "to feel" in the sense of expressing an opinion. It's one of the most common ways to share your thoughts or impressions about something.

**Structure:**
Subject + 觉得 + opinion/statement

**Examples:**
- 我觉得很好。(Wǒ juéde hěn hǎo.) — I think it's very good.
- 他觉得不好。(Tā juéde bù hǎo.) — He thinks it's not good.
- 你觉得呢？(Nǐ juéde ne?) — What do you think?

觉得 is typically followed by adjectives, clauses, or complete thoughts expressing your subjective impression.

## [phase:atomic] The phrase 你觉得怎么样？
tags: phase:atomic, question-form, asking-opinion

**你觉得怎么样？** (Nǐ juéde zěnmeyàng?) is a standard way to ask someone's opinion. It literally means "You think how?" but translates to "What do you think?" or "How do you find it?"

**Components:**
- 你 (nǐ) — you
- 觉得 (juéde) — think/feel
- 怎么样 (zěnmeyàng) — how/how about

This question is extremely versatile and can be used to ask about anything: food, movies, people, places, ideas, etc.

**Common responses:**
- 我觉得很好。(Wǒ juéde hěn hǎo.) — I think it's very good.
- 我觉得不错。(Wǒ juéde bú cuò.) — I think it's pretty good.
- 我觉得一般。(Wǒ juéde yìbān.) — I think it's just okay/average.

## [phase:atomic] The adjective 好吃 (hǎochī) — delicious
tags: phase:atomic, adjective, food

**好吃** (hǎochī) means "delicious" or "tasty." It literally breaks down as 好 (hǎo, good) + 吃 (chī, to eat) = "good to eat."

**Examples:**
- 很好吃。(Hěn hǎochī.) — Very delicious.
- 不好吃。(Bù hǎochī.) — Not delicious./Not tasty.
- 这个很好吃。(Zhège hěn hǎochī.) — This is very delicious.

好吃 is one of the most useful food-related adjectives and is used to compliment cooking or describe food you enjoy.

## [phase:atomic] The adjective 好看 (hǎokàn) — good-looking
tags: phase:atomic, adjective, appearance

**好看** (hǎokàn) means "good-looking," "attractive," or "nice to look at." It breaks down as 好 (hǎo, good) + 看 (kàn, to look/to see) = "good to look at."

**Examples:**
- 很好看。(Hěn hǎokàn.) — Very good-looking./Very attractive.
- 不好看。(Bù hǎokàn.) — Not good-looking./Not attractive.
- 这个电影很好看。(Zhège diànyǐng hěn hǎokàn.) — This movie is very good.

好看 can describe people, things, movies, clothing, scenery, or anything visual. When talking about movies or books, it means "good" or "interesting" rather than literally "attractive."

## [phase:atomic] The adjective 好听 (hǎotīng) — good-sounding
tags: phase:atomic, adjective, sound

**好听** (hǎotīng) means "good-sounding," "pleasant to listen to," or "nice-sounding." It breaks down as 好 (hǎo, good) + 听 (tīng, to listen/to hear) = "good to listen to."

**Examples:**
- 很好听。(Hěn hǎotīng.) — Very pleasant to listen to./Sounds very nice.
- 不好听。(Bù hǎotīng.) — Doesn't sound good./Unpleasant to listen to.
- 这个音乐很好听。(Zhège yīnyuè hěn hǎotīng.) — This music sounds very nice.

好听 is used for music, songs, voices, languages, names, and anything auditory.

## [phase:atomic] The pattern 好 + verb compounds
tags: phase:atomic, grammar-pattern

The character **好** (hǎo, good) can combine with verbs to create compound adjectives that mean "good to [verb]" or "easy to [verb]."

**Common examples:**
- 好吃 (hǎochī) — good to eat = delicious
- 好看 (hǎokàn) — good to look at = good-looking/attractive
- 好听 (hǎotīng) — good to listen to = good-sounding

**Pattern:** 好 + verb = "good/easy to [perform that action]"

This productive pattern helps you understand and remember these compounds as related concepts rather than isolated vocabulary items.

## [phase:atomic] Asking for opinions with 你喜欢吗？
tags: phase:atomic, question-form, preferences

**你喜欢吗？** (Nǐ xǐhuan ma?) means "Do you like it?" This is a direct yes/no question asking about someone's preference or liking.

**Note:** 喜欢 (xǐhuan, to like) was taught in subtopic 6.3. If you need to review this vocabulary, refer back to that section.

**Structure:**
你 (nǐ, you) + 喜欢 (xǐhuan, to like) + 吗 (ma, question particle)

**Common responses:**
- 喜欢。(Xǐhuan.) — Yes, I like it.
- 不喜欢。(Bù xǐhuan.) — No, I don't like it.
- 很喜欢。(Hěn xǐhuan.) — I like it very much.

This question is more specific than 你觉得怎么样？ because it asks specifically about liking, not just general opinion.

## [phase:atomic] Basic structure: Subject + 觉得 + adjective phrase
tags: phase:atomic, sentence-structure

When expressing opinions with 觉得, the basic sentence structure places the opinion holder as the subject, followed by 觉得, then the opinion itself (usually an adjective or adjective phrase).

**Structure:**
Subject + 觉得 + [adjective/adjective phrase/clause]

**Examples:**
- 我觉得很好。(Wǒ juéde hěn hǎo.) — I think it's very good.
- 他觉得好吃。(Tā juéde hǎochī.) — He thinks it's delicious.
- 妈妈觉得好看。(Māma juéde hǎokàn.) — Mom thinks it's good-looking.

The topic being evaluated is often understood from context and doesn't need to be explicitly stated in the sentence.

## [phase:complex] Expressing opinions about food
tags: phase:complex, practical-application, food

When discussing food preferences in Chinese, you combine opinion structures with food-specific adjectives. The most natural flow is to use 觉得 or 认为 with 好吃 or its negative.

**Common expressions:**
- 我觉得很好吃。(Wǒ juéde hěn hǎochī.) — I think it's very delicious.
- 你觉得好吃吗？(Nǐ juéde hǎochī ma?) — Do you think it's delicious?
- 我认为不好吃。(Wǒ rènwéi bù hǎochī.) — I think it's not delicious.

You can also ask opinions and respond:
- A: 你觉得怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zěnmeyàng?) — What do you think?
- B: 我觉得很好吃。(Wǒ juéde hěn hǎochī.) — I think it's very delicious.

## [phase:complex] Comparing 你觉得怎么样？ and 你喜欢吗？
tags: phase:complex, question-forms, pragmatics

These two questions ask for different types of feedback. Understanding when to use each makes your Chinese more natural.

**你觉得怎么样？** (What do you think?)
- Asks for general opinion/assessment
- Open-ended: allows various responses
- More objective-sounding
- Response: 我觉得 + opinion

**你喜欢吗？** (Do you like it?)
- Asks specifically about personal preference/liking
- Yes/no question
- More about personal taste
- Response: 喜欢/不喜欢

**Example context:**
After a movie, 你觉得怎么样？ asks for your assessment (good/bad/interesting), while 你喜欢吗？ asks whether you personally liked it (might be good but not your taste).

## [phase:complex] Using 不错 in opinion statements
tags: phase:complex, practical-application, assessment

不错 (pretty good/not bad) is frequently used with 觉得 and 认为 to give moderate positive assessments. It's polite and commonly used in Chinese conversation.

**Examples:**
- 我觉得不错。(Wǒ juéde bú cuò.) — I think it's pretty good.
- 你认为不错吗？(Nǐ rènwéi bú cuò ma?) — Do you think it's not bad?
- 这个我觉得不错。(Zhège wǒ juéde bú cuò.) — I think this is pretty good.

**Comparison:**
- 很好 (very good) — more enthusiastic
- 不错 (pretty good/not bad) — moderate, common
- 好 (good) — basic positive
- 一般 (average/so-so) — neutral
- 不好 (not good) — negative

## [phase:complex] Responding to opinion questions
tags: phase:complex, conversation, responses

When someone asks for your opinion, your response should match the question type and use appropriate structures.

**Response patterns:**

For 你觉得怎么样？:
- 我觉得 + [assessment]
- Examples: 很好 / 不错 / 很好吃 / 很好看 / 一般

For 你喜欢吗？:
- 喜欢 / 不喜欢
- Can add intensity: 很喜欢 / 不太喜欢

For 你觉得...吗？ (Do you think it's...?):
- Confirm or deny: 我觉得 + [adjective] / 我觉得不 + [adjective]
- Example: Q: 你觉得好吃吗？ A: 我觉得很好吃。/ 我觉得不好吃。

## [phase:integration] Giving and receiving compliments
tags: phase:integration, social-skills, culture

In Chinese culture, compliments often use 觉得 + adjective structures, and understanding how to give and graciously receive them is important.

**Note:** Some of the structures below use 得 (complement) and 的 (modification) patterns that are beyond HSK 1 level. These are presented as reference material for context only.

**Giving compliments:**
- 我觉得你很好看。(I think you look very nice/attractive.)
- 我觉得你做的菜很好吃。(I think the food you made is very delicious.) — *Note: uses 的 modification*
- 我觉得你唱得很好听。(I think you sing very well/it sounds very nice.) — *Note: uses 得 complement*

**Receiving compliments (typically modest):**
- Chinese speakers often downplay compliments rather than simply saying "thank you"
- Common responses: 哪里哪里 (nǎli nǎli, "not at all"), 一般 (yìbān, "just average"), or simple 谢谢 (xièxie, "thank you")

**Asking for feedback:**
- 你觉得我做的菜怎么样？(What do you think of the food I made?)
- 你觉得我唱得好听吗？(Do you think I sing well?)
