## [phase:atomic] The Copula 是 (shì)
tags: grammar, copula, basic-sentence

是 (shì) is the Chinese copula verb, equivalent to "to be" in English when linking two nouns or noun phrases. It establishes identity or category: X 是 Y means "X is Y."

Unlike English, 是 is only used to equate nouns. It is **not** used to link subjects directly to bare adjectives for description (you don't say *我是高兴 for "I am happy"), though it can be used with nominalized adjective phrases (e.g., 他是聪明的 'He is the smart one'). It is also not used to indicate location.

Basic structure: **Subject + 是 + Noun/Noun Phrase**

Example: 我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuéshēng.) — I am a student.

## [phase:atomic] Forming X是Y Sentences
tags: grammar, sentence-structure, affirmative

The basic affirmative sentence with 是 follows this pattern:

**Subject + 是 + Predicate Noun**

Examples:
- 我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuéshēng.) — I am a student.
- 他是老师。(Tā shì lǎoshī.) — He is a teacher.
- 这是书。(Zhè shì shū.) — This is a book.
- 那是一个苹果。(Nà shì yī ge píngguǒ.) — That is an apple. (Note: Measure words like 个 are commonly used with 是 sentences, though they may be omitted in casual speech.)

Notice that Chinese does not use articles like "a" or "the." The sentence structure is straightforward: simply place 是 between the subject and what you're identifying it as.

## [phase:atomic] Subject Pronouns with 是
tags: grammar, pronouns, sentence-formation

是 sentences commonly use personal pronouns as subjects:

- 我是⋯ (Wǒ shì...) — I am...
- 你是⋯ (Nǐ shì...) — You are...
- 他是⋯ (Tā shì...) — He is...
- 她是⋯ (Tā shì...) — She is...
- 它是⋯ (Tā shì...) — It is...
- 我们是⋯ (Wǒmen shì...) — We are...
- 他们是⋯ (Tāmen shì...) — They are...

Note: 是 does not change form based on the subject. Unlike English ("am/is/are"), 是 remains the same for all persons and numbers.

## [phase:atomic] Demonstratives with 是
tags: grammar, demonstratives, identification

Demonstrative pronouns frequently appear as subjects in 是 sentences:

These constructions are essential for introducing or identifying objects and people.

## [phase:atomic] Negating with 不是
tags: grammar, negation, sentence-structure

To negate a 是 sentence, place 不 (bù) directly before 是, forming 不是 (bù shì) — "is not" or "am not."

Structure: **Subject + 不是 + Noun**

Examples:
- 我不是老师。(Wǒ bù shì lǎoshī.) — I am not a teacher.
- 他不是学生。(Tā bù shì xuéshēng.) — He is not a student.
- 这不是我的书。(Zhè bù shì wǒ de shū.) — This is not my book.

Important: The negation word for 是 is always 不, never 没. Remember: 不是 = "is not."

## [phase:atomic] The Negation Word 不 with 是
tags: grammar, negation, vocabulary

不 (bù) is the standard negation word used with 是. It is placed directly before 是 to form the negative.

Key points:
- 不 is pronounced "bù" in isolation and before most syllables
- Before another fourth tone syllable (like the fourth tone of 是), 不 changes to second tone (bú). This tone change is reflected in some pinyin systems, though it is often not written in standard pinyin notation.
- 不 is never separated from 是 — they form a unit: 不是

Do **not** use 没 (méi) with 是. 没 is used with other verbs to indicate absence or non-completion, but never with 是.

## [phase:atomic] Yes-No Questions with 吗
tags: grammar, questions, sentence-structure

To form a yes-no question with 是, add the question particle 吗 (ma) at the end of the statement.

Structure: **Subject + 是 + Noun + 吗？**

Examples:
- 你是学生吗？(Nǐ shì xuéshēng ma?) — Are you a student?
- 他是老师吗？(Tā shì lǎoshī ma?) — Is he a teacher?
- 这是你的书吗？(Zhè shì nǐ de shū ma?) — Is this your book?

The sentence order remains the same as in a statement — simply add 吗 at the end. The question particle always comes last, after the entire statement.

## [phase:atomic] Answering 是 Questions: Affirmative
tags: grammar, questions, responses

To answer "yes" to a 是 question, you can respond with:

1. **是。** (Shì.) — Yes. / (It) is.
2. **是的。** (Shì de.) — Yes. (slightly more formal/complete)
3. **Full sentence repetition**: 我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuéshēng.) — I am a student.

The simplest and most common response is just 是 or 是的.

Example exchange:
- Q: 你是学生吗？(Nǐ shì xuéshēng ma?) — Are you a student?
- A: 是。(Shì.) — Yes.

## [phase:atomic] Answering 是 Questions: Negative
tags: grammar, questions, responses

To answer "no" to a 是 question, respond with:

1. **不是。** (Bù shì.) — No. / (It) is not.
2. **Full negative sentence**: 我不是老师。(Wǒ bù shì lǎoshī.) — I am not a teacher.

The most common response is simply 不是.

Example exchange:
- Q: 他是学生吗？(Tā shì xuéshēng ma?) — Is he a student?
- A: 不是。(Bù shì.) — No.

Notice the parallel: affirmative questions answered with 是, negative questions answered with 不是. Chinese question-answer patterns are very logical.

## [phase:atomic] Word Order in 是 Sentences
tags: grammar, sentence-structure, word-order

Chinese word order with 是 is consistent and straightforward:

**Statement**: Subject + 是 + Noun
**Negative**: Subject + 不是 + Noun
**Question**: Subject + 是 + Noun + 吗？

The word order does **not** change when forming questions — simply add 吗 at the end. This differs from English, where word order inverts ("You are" → "Are you").

Examples:
- 他是学生。(Statement)
- 他不是学生。(Negative)
- 他是学生吗？(Question)

## [phase:complex] Combining 是 with Modifiers
tags: grammar, modifiers, sentence-complexity

是 sentences can include modifiers before the predicate noun, such as possessives (的 phrases) or other descriptive phrases.

Examples:
- 他是我的老师。(Tā shì wǒ de lǎoshī.) — He is my teacher.
- 这是中国人。(Zhè shì Zhōngguó rén.) — This is a Chinese person.
- 她是很好的学生。(Tā shì hěn hǎo de xuéshēng.) — She is a very good student.

The structure remains: Subject + 是 + [Modified Noun Phrase]

The entire noun phrase after 是 functions as the predicate. Modifiers and possessives come before the main noun.

## [phase:complex] Responding to 是 Questions with Clarification
tags: grammar, conversation, responses

When answering 是 questions, you can provide clarification by giving the correct information after saying 不是:

- Q: 他是老师吗？(Tā shì lǎoshī ma?) — Is he a teacher?
- A: 不是，他是学生。(Bù shì, tā shì xuéshēng.) — No, he is a student.

Structure: **不是，[Subject] + 是 + [Correct Information]**

This pattern allows you to correct misunderstandings while providing accurate information. The comma after 不是 indicates a pause before the clarification.

## [phase:integration] Real-World Introductions

## [phase:integration] Common Mistakes with 是
tags: grammar, error-correction, learning-strategies

Learners commonly make these errors with 是:

**1. Using 是 with bare adjectives:**
❌ 我是高兴。(Incorrect)
✓ 我很高兴。(Correct) — Chinese uses stative verbs (like 很 + adjective) where English uses "be + adjective." The structure 很 + adjective is the standard way to express descriptive qualities, not 是 + adjective.

**2. Using 是 for location:**
❌ 我是在家。(Incorrect)
✓ 我在家。(Correct) — "I am at home" uses 在, not 是

**3. Using 没 instead of 不 for negation:**
❌ 我没是学生。(Incorrect)
✓ 我不是学生。(Correct)

**4. Wrong question formation:**
❌ 是你学生吗？(Incorrect)
✓ 你是学生吗？(Correct)

Remember: 是 = identity/category between two nouns only.
