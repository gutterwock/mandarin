## [phase:atomic] 来 (lái) – come

tags: movement, direction, basic-verb

The verb 来 (lái) means "to come" – movement toward the speaker or toward a reference point.

Basic structure:
- 我**来** (wǒ **lái**) – I come
- 你**来** (nǐ **lái**) – you come
- 他**来** (tā **lái**) – he comes

来 indicates motion toward "here" from the speaker's perspective.

Examples:
- 你来！ (nǐ lái!) – Come! (you come toward me)
- 她来了 (tā lái le) – She came / She's here
- 我来中国 (wǒ lái Zhōngguó) – I come to China

## [phase:atomic] 去 (qù) – go

tags: movement, direction, basic-verb

The verb 去 (qù) means "to go" – movement away from the speaker or away from a reference point.

Basic structure:
- 我**去** (wǒ **qù**) – I go
- 你**去** (nǐ **qù**) – you go
- 他**去** (tā **qù**) – he goes

去 indicates motion away from "here" from the speaker's perspective.

Examples:
- 我去 (wǒ qù) – I'm going / I'll go
- 你去吗？ (nǐ qù ma?) – Are you going?
- 他去了 (tā qù le) – He went / He's gone

## [phase:atomic] 去 + place (destination structure)

tags: movement, destination, sentence-pattern

To express "go to [place]", use: **去 + place**

Structure: Subject + 去 + Place

Examples:
- 我**去学校** (wǒ **qù xuéxiào**) – I go to school
- 他**去医院** (tā **qù yīyuàn**) – He goes to the hospital
- 你**去图书馆** (nǐ **qù túshūguǎn**) – You go to the library

No additional particle is needed between 去 and the place word.

## [phase:atomic] 来 + place (destination structure)

Structure: Subject + 来 + Place

## [phase:atomic] 回 (huí) – return

tags: movement, direction, basic-verb

The verb 回 (huí) means "to return" or "to go back" – movement back to an origin point.

Basic structure:
- 我**回** (wǒ **huí**) – I return
- 你**回家** (nǐ **huí jiā**) – you return home
- 他**回** (tā **huí**) – he returns

回 is commonly used with 家 (home): 回家 (huí jiā) – return home / go home

Examples:
- 我回家 (wǒ huí jiā) – I go home / I return home
- 你回！ (nǐ huí!) – Go back!
- 他回中国 (tā huí Zhōngguó) – He returns to China

## [phase:atomic] Directional complement: verb + 来

tags: directional-complement, compound-verb

A directional complement adds direction information to a verb. **Verb + 来** indicates movement toward the speaker.

Common patterns:
- **进来** (jìn lái) – come in (enter toward speaker)
- **出来** (chū lái) – come out (exit toward speaker)
- **上来** (shàng lái) – come up (ascend toward speaker)
- **下来** (xià lái) – come down (descend toward speaker)

The 来 component means the action moves toward "here".

Examples:
- 你进来 (nǐ jìn lái) – You come in
- 他出来了 (tā chū lái le) – He came out

## [phase:atomic] Contrast: 来 vs 去 with destinations

tags: direction-contrast, sentence-pattern

When using destinations with 来 and 去, choose based on the speaker's perspective:

**去 + place** = going away from current location to that place
**来 + place** = coming toward current location / reference point

Examples from speaker at home:
- 我**去**学校 (wǒ **qù** xuéxiào) – I go to school (leaving home)
- 你**来**我家 (nǐ **lái** wǒ jiā) – You come to my home (toward me)

Examples from speaker at school:
- 我**来**学校 (wǒ **lái** xuéxiào) – I come to school (arriving here)
- 我**去**图书馆 (wǒ **qù** túshūguǎn) – I go to the library (leaving school)

## [phase:complex] Directional complements with location context

tags: directional-complement, context, spatial-reasoning

The choice between 来 and 去 in directional complements depends on speaker position and perspective.

Scenario: Speaker is outside a room.
- 他**进去**了 (tā **jìn qù** le) – He went in (away from speaker, into room)
- 你**出来**！ (nǐ **chū lái**!) – Come out! (toward speaker, from room)

Scenario: Speaker is inside a room.
- 你**进来** (nǐ **jìn lái**) – Come in (toward speaker, into room)
- 我**出去** (wǒ **chū qù**) – I'm going out (away from speaker, from room)

The same physical action (entering a room) uses different words based on where the speaker stands.

## [phase:complex] Movement with 回 (return patterns)

tags: return, destination, context

回 (huí) expresses returning to an origin point. It commonly pairs with:
- **回家** (huí jiā) – return home / go home
- **回 + country** – return to one's home country
- **回 + origin location** – return to where you're from

Context matters:
- 我**回**中国 (wǒ **huí** Zhōngguó) – I return to China (implies a connection to China: home country, origin, or previously visited)
- 我**去**中国 (wǒ **qù** Zhōngguó) – I go to China (neutral direction)

回 implies a connection or previous presence at that location.

## [phase:complex] Polite invitation with 请 + movement verb

tags: politeness, invitation, pragmatics

请 (qǐng) + movement verb creates polite invitations or requests.

Common patterns:
- **请进** (qǐng jìn) – Please come in
- **请来** (qǐng lái) – Please come
- **请进来** (qǐng jìn lái) – Please come in (more specific direction)

These are standard hospitality phrases in Chinese.

Example context:
- Someone knocks at your door
- You open it and say: 请进！(qǐng jìn!)

## [phase:integration] Giving directions with movement verbs

tags: directions, integration, practical-application

Movement verbs combine to give clear directions about where to go and how to enter/exit.

Example directions:
- 你去学校，进去，上楼 (nǐ qù xuéxiào, jìn qù, shàng lóu) – You go to school, go in, go upstairs
- 请来我家，进来，上来 (qǐng lái wǒ jiā, jìn lái, shàng lái) – Please come to my home, come in, come up

Notice how:
- Main destination uses 去/来
- Entry/exit uses 进/出 + 来/去
- Vertical movement uses 上/下 + 来/去
