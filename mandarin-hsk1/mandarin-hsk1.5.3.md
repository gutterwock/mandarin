## [phase:atomic] Personal Pronouns - 我 (I/me)
tags: pronouns, basic-vocabulary

The pronoun **我** (wǒ) means "I" or "me" in English. It is one of the most essential words in Mandarin Chinese.

**Pronunciation:** wǒ (third tone - falling then rising)

**Usage:**
- 我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuésheng.) - I am a student.
- 我喝水。(Wǒ hē shuǐ.) - I drink water.

The character 我 is used in both subject and object positions, unlike English which distinguishes "I" from "me."

## [phase:atomic] Personal Pronouns - 你 (you)
tags: pronouns, basic-vocabulary

The pronoun **你** (nǐ) means "you" (singular) in English. It is used when addressing one person informally.

**Pronunciation:** nǐ (third tone - falling then rising)

**Usage:**
- 你好！(Nǐ hǎo!) - Hello! (literally "You good!")
- 你是老师吗？(Nǐ shì lǎoshī ma?) - Are you a teacher?

**Polite form:** For formal or respectful contexts, use **您** (nín), which is pronounced the same as 你 but with a higher, more formal register. Example: 您好！(Nín hǎo!) - Hello! (formal).

## [phase:atomic] Personal Pronouns - 他 (he/him)
tags: pronouns, basic-vocabulary

The pronoun **他** (tā) means "he" or "him" in English. It is used to refer to a male person.

**Pronunciation:** tā (first tone - high and level)

**Usage:**
- 他是我朋友。(Tā shì wǒ péngyou.) - He is my friend.
- 他喝茶。(Tā hē chá.) - He drinks tea.

Note that 他, 她 (she), and 它 (it) are all pronounced identically as "tā" but use different characters to indicate gender or whether referring to a person or thing.

## [phase:atomic] Plural Pronouns - 们 suffix
tags: pronouns, grammar

The suffix **们** (men) is added to singular pronouns to make them plural. It has a neutral tone (no specific tone marking).

**Pronunciation:** men (neutral/light tone)

**Formation:**
- 我 (I) + 们 = 我们 (wǒmen) - we/us
- 你 (you) + 们 = 你们 (nǐmen) - you (plural)
- 他 (he) + 们 = 他们 (tāmen) - they/them (male or mixed group)
- 她 (she) + 们 = 她们 (tāmen) - they/them (all female)

**Usage:**
- 我们是学生。(Wǒmen shì xuésheng.) - We are students.
- 你们好！(Nǐmen hǎo!) - Hello (to multiple people)!

## [phase:atomic] Possessive Particle - 的 structure
tags: grammar, possessives

The particle **的** (de) is used to show possession in Chinese, similar to "'s" in English or "of" in other constructions.

**Pronunciation:** de (neutral tone)

**Basic structure:** Possessor + 的 + Possessed item

**Examples:**
- 我的朋友 (wǒ de péngyou) - my friend
- 他的妈妈 (tā de māma) - his mother
- 老师的书 (lǎoshī de shū) - the teacher's book

**Note:** 的 is often omitted between pronouns and close family members:
- 我妈妈 (wǒ māma) - my mom (的 omitted)
- 你爸爸 (nǐ bàba) - your dad (的 omitted)

## [phase:complex] Pronoun System Overview
tags: pronouns, grammar, system

Chinese personal pronouns follow a systematic pattern. Understanding the complete system helps you use pronouns accurately.

**Singular → Plural pattern:**

| Singular | Pinyin | Plural | Pinyin | Meaning |
|----------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| 我 | wǒ | 我们 | wǒmen | I/me → we/us |
| 你 | nǐ | 你们 | nǐmen | you (sg.) → you (pl.) |
| 他 | tā | 他们 | tāmen | he/him → they/them (male/mixed) |
| 她 | tā | 她们 | tāmen | she/her → they/them (female) |

**Key observations:**
- All three third-person pronouns (他/她) sound identical: "tā"
- The suffix 们 (men) always makes pronouns plural
- Written Chinese distinguishes gender in third person; spoken Chinese does not
- In practice, 他们 is often used for mixed-gender groups
- Note: The pronoun 它 (it, for non-human objects) also exists but is introduced at higher HSK levels

## [phase:complex] Sibling Terms System
tags: family, kinship-terms, cultural-context

Chinese sibling terms are more specific than English. They simultaneously indicate both gender and age relative to the speaker, making family relationships clearer.

**The four sibling terms:**

| Chinese | Pinyin | Gender | Age relative to speaker |
|---------|--------|--------|------------------------|
| 哥哥 | gēge | male | older |
| 姐姐 | jiějie | female | older |
| 弟弟 | dìdi | male | younger |
| 妹妹 | mèimei | female | younger |

**Cultural note:** These terms can also be used as respectful or friendly forms of address for non-siblings (e.g., calling an older male friend 哥哥 or a slightly older female acquaintance 姐姐).

- 他的姐姐是医生。(Tā de jiějie shì yīshēng.) - His older sister is a doctor.

## [phase:complex] Family Descriptions with 有
tags: family, sentence-patterns, grammar

To describe your family members, use the verb 有 (yǒu, "to have") combined with measure words and family terms.

**Basic pattern:** Subject + 有 + Number + 个 + Family member

- **和 (hé)** - and (connects nouns) [NEW vocabulary introduced here]

**Note:** The measure word 个 (ge) is used with people and many other nouns.

## [phase:complex] Possessives with 的
tags: grammar, possessives, sentence-patterns

The particle 的 (de) creates possessive relationships, but its usage has nuances that learners must understand.

**When to use 的:**
- With non-family relationships: 我的朋友 (wǒ de péngyou) - my friend
- With formal relationships: 老师的书 (lǎoshī de shū) - the teacher's book
- For emphasis or clarity: 我的爸爸 (wǒ de bàba) - my (own) father (emphatic)

**When 的 is often omitted:**
- With immediate family members: 我妈妈 (wǒ māma) - my mom
- With very close relationships: 我家 (wǒ jiā) - my home

**Complex examples:**
- 我妈妈的朋友 (wǒ māma de péngyou) - my mom's friend
- 他的弟弟 (tā de dìdi) - his younger brother (的 adds clarity)
- 老师的哥哥 (lǎoshī de gēge) - the teacher's older brother

## [phase:complex] Social Roles and Relationships
tags: relationships, social-terms, cultural-context

Understanding the distinction between different social relationship terms helps you navigate Chinese social contexts appropriately.

**Key relationship terms:**
- **朋友 (péngyou)** - friend: A general term for people you know socially
- **同学 (tóngxué)** - classmate: Specifically people who study/studied with you
- **老师 (lǎoshī)** - teacher: An authority figure in educational settings
- **医生 (yīshēng)** - doctor: A professional medical practitioner

**Important distinctions:**
- 同学 and 朋友 overlap but aren't identical: all close classmates might be friends, but not all friends are classmates
- 老师 can be used as a respectful title: "李老师" (Teacher Li)
- Occupational terms (医生, 老师) can be combined with possessives: 我的医生 (my doctor)

**Example sentences:**
