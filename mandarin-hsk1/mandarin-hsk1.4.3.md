# 有 (yǒu): Possession and Existence

## [phase:atomic] Basic function of 有
tags: grammar, possession

The verb 有 (yǒu) is one of the most fundamental verbs in Mandarin Chinese. It primarily expresses two meanings: possession ("to have") and existence ("there is/are"). Unlike most Chinese verbs, 有 has special negation rules that make it unique.

## [phase:atomic] Possession with 有
tags: grammar, possession

To express possession in Mandarin, use the pattern: **Subject + 有 + Object**

Examples:
- 我有书。(Wǒ yǒu shū.) — I have a book/books.
- 她有电脑。(Tā yǒu diànnǎo.) — She has a computer.
- 他们有车。(Tāmen yǒu chē.) — They have a car.

The object can include measure words and numbers for specificity: 我有一本书 (Wǒ yǒu yì běn shū) — I have one book.

## [phase:atomic] Existence with 有
tags: grammar, existence

有 also expresses existence, meaning "there is" or "there are." The pattern is: **Location + 有 + Object**

Examples:
- 桌子上有书。(Zhuōzi shang yǒu shū.) — There is a book on the table.
- 教室里有学生。(Jiàoshì lǐ yǒu xuésheng.) — There are students in the classroom.
- 家里有人。(Jiā lǐ yǒu rén.) — There is someone at home.

The location phrase comes first, indicating where something exists.

## [phase:atomic] Negating 有 with 没有
tags: grammar, negation

Unlike most verbs that are negated with 不 (bù), 有 is negated with 没 (méi) or 没有 (méiyǒu). Never use 不有.

Examples:
- 我没有钱。(Wǒ méiyǒu qián.) — I don't have money.
- 她没有哥哥。(Tā méiyǒu gēge.) — She doesn't have an older brother.
- 桌子上没有书。(Zhuōzi shang méiyǒu shū.) — There are no books on the table.

Both 没 and 没有 work for negation, with 没有 being more emphatic.

## [phase:atomic] 有 vs 是 (shì)
tags: grammar, comparison

有 (to have) and 是 (to be) serve different functions and cannot be interchanged:
- 有 = possession or existence
- 是 = identity or definition

Compare:
- 我有书。(Wǒ yǒu shū.) — I have a book.
- 我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuésheng.) — I am a student.

## [phase:atomic] Adding quantity with 有
tags: grammar, numbers

有 combines naturally with numbers and measure words to specify quantity: **Subject + 有 + Number + Measure Word + Noun**

Examples:
- 我有一个朋友。(Wǒ yǒu yí ge péngyou.) — I have one friend.
- 她有三本书。(Tā yǒu sān běn shū.) — She has three books.
- 教室里有二十个学生。(Jiàoshì lǐ yǒu èrshí ge xuésheng.) — There are twenty students in the classroom.

## [phase:atomic] Word order in existence sentences
tags: grammar, word-order

For existence, the location must come before 有. The pattern is fixed: **Location Word + 有 + Object**

Correct: 桌子上有杯子。(Zhuōzi shang yǒu bēizi.) — There is a cup on the table.
Incorrect: 有杯子桌子上。

This differs from possession sentences where the subject (person) comes first.

## [phase:complex] Distinguishing possession and existence
tags: grammar, application

Context determines whether 有 means possession or existence:
- If the subject is a person/entity, 有 typically means possession: 我有电脑 (I have a computer)
- If the subject is a location, 有 typically means existence: 桌子上有电脑 (There is a computer on the table)

Compare:
- 我有一只狗。(Wǒ yǒu yì zhī gǒu.) — I have a dog. [possession]
- 家里有一只狗。(Jiā lǐ yǒu yì zhī gǒu.) — There is a dog at home. [existence]

## [phase:complex] Negation in different contexts
tags: grammar, negation, application

没有 negates both possession and existence. The sentence structure remains the same, just add 没有 in place of 有:

Possession:
- 我有车。→ 我没有车。(I have a car. → I don't have a car.)

Existence:
- 桌子上有书。→ 桌子上没有书。(There are books on the table. → There are no books on the table.)

## [phase:complex] Questions with 有
tags: grammar, questions

To ask yes/no questions with 有, add 吗 (ma) at the end, or use the A-not-A pattern with 有没有:

Method 1 (吗):
- 你有手机吗?(Nǐ yǒu shǒujī ma?) — Do you have a phone?

Method 2 (有没有):
- 你有没有手机?(Nǐ yǒu méiyǒu shǒujī?) — Do you have a phone?

Both are equally correct and common.

## [phase:complex] Combining 有 with other elements
tags: grammar, application

有 sentences can include time words, adjectives, and other modifiers:

With time:
- 今天我有课。(Jīntiān wǒ yǒu kè.) — I have class today.

With adjectives (describing what is possessed):
- 我有一个好朋友。(Wǒ yǒu yí ge hǎo péngyou.) — I have a good friend.

With location + quantity:
- 桌子上有三本书。(Zhuōzi shang yǒu sān běn shū.) — There are three books on the table.

## [phase:complex] Common mistakes with 有
tags: grammar, errors

Common errors to avoid:

1. Using 不 instead of 没: ❌ 我不有车 → ✓ 我没有车
2. Confusing 有 and 是: ❌ 我有学生 (meaning "I am a student") → ✓ 我是学生
3. Wrong word order in existence: ❌ 有书桌子上 → ✓ 桌子上有书
4. Omitting location words: ❌ 有猫 (incomplete for existence) → ✓ 家里有猫

## [phase:integration] Real-world possession scenarios
tags: application, conversation

有 is essential for discussing daily life and possessions. In conversations, you'll use it to talk about what you own, what you need, and what's available:

Shopping: 你有这本书吗?(Nǐ yǒu zhè běn shū ma?) — Do you have this book?
Family: 我有两个姐姐。(Wǒ yǒu liǎng ge jiějie.) — I have two older sisters.
Work: 明天我有会。(Míngtiān wǒ yǒu huì.) — I have a meeting tomorrow.

## [phase:integration] Describing locations and availability
tags: application, existence

Use 有 to describe what's available in locations — essential for giving directions, describing places, or explaining what facilities exist:

- 这儿有医院吗?(Zhèr yǒu yīyuàn ma?) — Is there a hospital here?
- 附近有饭馆。(Fùjìn yǒu fànguǎn.) — There are restaurants nearby.
- 房间里没有人。(Fángjiān lǐ méiyǒu rén.) — There's no one in the room.

## [phase:integration] Contrasting 有, 是, and 在
tags: grammar, integration, comparison

Three verbs often confused by beginners:
- 有 = to have / there is
- 是 = to be (identity)
- 在 = to be at (location)

Examples:
- 我有书。(I have a book.) [possession]
- 我是学生。(I am a student.) [identity]
- 我在家。(I am at home.) [location]
- 家里有书。(There are books at home.) [existence]
