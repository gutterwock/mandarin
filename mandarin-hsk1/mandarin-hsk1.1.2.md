# Tone Sandhi and Tone Rules

## [phase:atomic] Third Tone Sandhi: The Basic Rule
tags: tone-sandhi, third-tone, pronunciation

When two third-tone syllables appear together in Mandarin, the first third tone changes to a second tone in pronunciation, though the tone mark remains written as third tone.

**Written:** nǐ (3rd) + hǎo (3rd) = 你好 nǐhǎo
**Pronounced:** ní (2nd) + hǎo (3rd) = 你好 níhǎo

This sandhi (tone change) happens automatically in natural speech. Native speakers apply it without conscious thought, but learners must practice to make it feel natural.

## [phase:atomic] Third Tone Sandhi: Written vs. Spoken
tags: tone-sandhi, third-tone, pinyin

An important distinction: tone sandhi affects pronunciation but NOT written pinyin. You always write the original tone marks (nǐhǎo), even though you pronounce the changed tones (níhǎo).

This means learners must remember both:
- **What to write:** original tones (3rd + 3rd)
- **What to say:** changed tones (2nd + 3rd)

Textbooks and dictionaries always show the original tones, not the sandhi pronunciation.

## [phase:atomic] The Negative 不 (bù): Base Tone
tags: bu-tone-change, negation, tone-rules

The negative word 不 (bù) normally has fourth tone. You can see this in many common words:

- 不对 bùduì (not correct)
- 不好 bùhǎo (not good)

When 不 appears before another fourth-tone syllable, it changes to second tone. This is a special tone change rule we'll cover next.

## [phase:atomic] The Negative 不 (bù): Tone Change Rule
tags: bu-tone-change, tone-rules, pronunciation

When 不 (bù, 4th tone) appears before another fourth-tone syllable, it changes to second tone in pronunciation (bú).

**The rule:** 4th + 4th → 2nd + 4th

Examples:
- 不是 bú shì (am/is/are not) — 不 changes to 2nd before shì (4th tone)
- 不去 bú qù (not go) — 不 changes to 2nd before qù (4th tone)
- 不要 bú yào (don't want) — 不 changes to 2nd before yào (4th tone)

But: 不好 bùhǎo (not good) — stays bù because hǎo is 3rd tone, not 4th.

## [phase:atomic] The Number 一 (yī): Base Tone
tags: yi-tone-change, numbers, tone-rules

The number 一 (one) normally has first tone: yī. You use this tone when:
- Counting: 一, 二, 三, 四... (yī, èr, sān, sì...)
- Saying the number alone: "一" (yī)
- Using it as an ordinal: 第一 dìyī (first)

However, when 一 appears before other syllables in words and phrases, it often changes tone.

## [phase:atomic] The Number 一 (yī): Tone Changes Before 4th Tone
tags: yi-tone-change, tone-rules, pronunciation

When 一 appears before a fourth-tone syllable, it changes to second tone (yí).

**The rule:** 1st + 4th → 2nd + 4th

Examples:
- 一个 yí gè (one [measure word])
- 一样 yí yàng (same)
- 一半 yí bàn (half)

This is similar to the 不 rule, but notice: 一 changes before 4th tone (becoming 2nd), while 不 itself IS 4th tone and also changes before 4th tone.

## [phase:atomic] The Number 一 (yī): Tone Changes Before 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Tones
tags: yi-tone-change, tone-rules, pronunciation

When 一 appears before a first, second, or third-tone syllable, it changes to fourth tone (yì).

**The rule:** 1st + (1st/2nd/3rd) → 4th + (1st/2nd/3rd)

Examples:
- 一天 yì tiān (one day) — before 1st tone
- 一年 yì nián (one year) — before 2nd tone
- 一起 yì qǐ (together) — before 3rd tone

Notice the pattern: 一 changes to 2nd before 4th, and changes to 4th before everything else (1st, 2nd, 3rd).

## [phase:atomic] Summary of 一 (yī) Tone Changes
tags: yi-tone-change, tone-rules, summary

Here's the complete pattern for 一:

**Base tone (1st):** When counting or standing alone
- 一, 二, 三... (yī, èr, sān...)

**Changes to 2nd tone:** Before 4th-tone syllables
- 一个 yí gè

**Changes to 4th tone:** Before 1st, 2nd, or 3rd-tone syllables
- 一天 yì tiān (before 1st)
- 一年 yì nián (before 2nd)
- 一起 yì qǐ (before 3rd)

Memory tip: Think of 一 as "agreeing to disagree" — it becomes the opposite register (2nd before 4th, 4th before others).

## [phase:atomic] Tone Sandhi in Pinyin Writing
tags: tone-sandhi, pinyin, writing-conventions

Important: Like third-tone sandhi, the tone changes for 不 and 一 affect pronunciation only, not standard pinyin writing.

**Always write:**
- 不是 bùshì (not bú shì)
- 一个 yīgè (not yí gè)
- 一天 yītiān (not yì tiān)

However, some textbooks DO write the changed tones (bú shì, yí gè) to help beginners learn pronunciation. But dictionaries, official documents, and standardized pinyin always use original tones.

## [phase:complex] Applying Multiple Tone Rules
tags: tone-sandhi, tone-rules, combined-rules

Real Chinese phrases often require applying multiple tone rules. You must identify each rule independently:

Example: 我不去 (I'm not going)
- 我 wǒ (3rd tone)
- 不 bù → bú (changes before 4th tone)
- 去 qù (4th tone)
- Result: wǒ bú qù

Example: 一百 (one hundred)
- 一 yī → no change (百 bǎi is 3rd tone... wait, that means 一 should become 4th!)
- 百 bǎi (3rd tone)
- Result: yì bǎi

Always check: Does third-tone sandhi apply? Does 不 come before 4th tone? Does 一 come before any tone?

c: nǐ bú hǎo (3rd + 2nd + 3rd)
d: ní bú hǎo (2nd + 2nd + 3rd)

## [phase:complex] Third-Tone Sandhi in Three-Syllable Phrases
tags: tone-sandhi, third-tone, complex-phrases

When three or more third tones appear together, apply sandhi from left to right in pairs:

Example: 你好吗 (How are you?)
- 你 nǐ (3rd)
- 好 hǎo (3rd)
- 吗 ma (neutral)

Step 1: 你 + 好 → ní + hǎo (first pair)
Step 2: hǎo + ma → hǎo + ma (好 stays 3rd because ma is neutral)
Result: ní hǎo ma

If all three were 3rd tone: 你好我
- 你好 → níhǎo
- 好我 → háowǒ
Result: ní háo wǒ

The grouping can vary based on meaning and natural pauses.

### question singleChoice difficulty:3

In 我也好 (I'm also good), how many third tones change to second tone?
a: None
b: One (我)
c: Two (我 and 也)
d: All three become second tone
answer: c
explanation: 我 (wǒ) changes to wó before 也 (yě, also 3rd tone), and 也 (yě) changes to yé before 好 (hǎo, also 3rd tone). We apply third-tone sandhi from left to right: first pair 我+也 → wó+yě, then second pair 也+好 → yé+hǎo. Result: wó yé hǎo.

• Final pronunciation: ní háo wǒ

## [phase:complex] Distinguishing 不 and 一 Tone Rules
tags: bu-tone-change, yi-tone-change, comparison

不 and 一 have similar but distinct tone rules:

**不 (base: 4th tone)**
- Changes to 2nd tone only before 4th tone
- Stays 4th tone before 1st, 2nd, 3rd tones

**一 (base: 1st tone)**
- Changes to 2nd tone before 4th tone
- Changes to 4th tone before 1st, 2nd, 3rd tones

Both change to 2nd before 4th, but 一 also changes before other tones (while 不 doesn't).

## [phase:complex] Tone Changes in Fast Speech
tags: tone-sandhi, pronunciation, natural-speech

In natural, fast speech, Chinese speakers may apply tone rules more liberally:

**Standard:** Only the first of two 3rd tones changes
**Fast speech:** Sometimes both may become "half-third" (a low tone that doesn't rise)

**Standard:** 不 and 一 follow strict rules
**Fast speech:** Syllables may reduce to neutral tone

For HSK 1 learners: Focus on the standard rules. Understanding that native speakers vary in fast speech helps you recognize different pronunciations, but you should apply the rules consistently.

## [phase:complex] Recognition vs. Production
tags: tone-sandhi, learning-strategy

At HSK 1 level, distinguish between:

**Recognition (listening):** You need to recognize that 不是 may sound like "bú shì" even when written "bùshì"

**Production (speaking):** You should actively apply tone rules when speaking, saying "bú shì" not "bù shì"

**Writing:** Always write original tones (bùshì, yīgè) unless specifically instructed otherwise by your textbook

Practice production rules explicitly, but develop flexible recognition skills.
