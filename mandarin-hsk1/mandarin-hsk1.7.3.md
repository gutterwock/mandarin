## [phase:atomic] Question word 什么 (shénme) - what
tags: question-words, what, grammar

The question word **什么** (shénme, "what") stays in the same position where the answer would appear. It does not move to the front of the sentence.

**Structure:** Replace the unknown element with 什么

Examples:
- 这是**什么**？ (Zhè shì **shénme**?) — "This is **what**?" → "What is this?"
- 你叫**什么**名字？ (Nǐ jiào **shénme** míngzi?) — "You are called **what** name?" → "What is your name?"
- 你喜欢**什么**？ (Nǐ xǐhuan **shénme**?) — "You like **what**?" → "What do you like?"

Notice that 什么 appears where the answer would be, not at the beginning.

## [phase:atomic] Question word 谁 (shéi/shuí) - who
tags: question-words, who, grammar

The question word **谁** (shéi, "who") replaces the person in the sentence. Like 什么, it stays in the position of the answer.

**Structure:** Replace the unknown person with 谁

Examples:
- **谁**是老师？ (**Shéi** shì lǎoshī?) — "**Who** is teacher?" → "Who is the teacher?"
- 你是**谁**？ (Nǐ shì **shéi**?) — "You are **who**?" → "Who are you?"
- **谁**喜欢他？ (**Shéi** xǐhuan tā?) — "**Who** likes him?" → "Who likes him?"

谁 can appear as the subject (beginning) or object (after verb), depending on what you're asking.

## [phase:atomic] Question word 哪 (nǎ) - which
tags: question-words, which, grammar

The question word **哪** (nǎ, "which") is always followed by a measure word and noun. It asks "which one" among options.

**Structure:** 哪 + measure word + noun

Examples:
- 你要**哪个**？ (Nǐ yào **nǎge**?) — "You want **which one**?"
- 他是**哪国**人？ (Tā shì **nǎ guó** rén?) — "He is **which country** person?" → "What nationality is he?"
- **哪本**书？ (**Nǎ běn** shū?) — "**Which (measure word)** book?"

Common pattern: 哪个 (which one) uses the general measure word 个.

## [phase:atomic] Question words 哪里/哪儿 (nǎlǐ/nǎr) - where
tags: question-words, where, location

The question words **哪里** (nǎlǐ) and **哪儿** (nǎr) both mean "where". 哪儿 is more common in northern China; 哪里 is used everywhere.

**Structure:** 哪里/哪儿 appears where the location would be

Examples:
- 你在**哪里**？ (Nǐ zài **nǎlǐ**?) — "You at **where**?" → "Where are you?"
- 你去**哪儿**？ (Nǐ qù **nǎr**?) — "You go **where**?" → "Where are you going?"
- 厕所在**哪里**？ (Cèsuǒ zài **nǎlǐ**?) — "Toilet at **where**?" → "Where is the toilet?"

Both forms are equally correct. Choose based on regional preference or personal habit.

## [phase:atomic] Question word 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) - when
tags: question-words, when, time

The phrase **什么时候** (shénme shíhou, "what time") literally means "what time" and asks "when". It can appear before or after the subject.

**Structure:** 什么时候 + action OR Subject + 什么时候 + action

Examples:
- 你**什么时候**去？ (Nǐ **shénme shíhou** qù?) — "You **when** go?" → "When do you go?"
- **什么时候**开始？ (**Shénme shíhou** kāishǐ?) — "**When** start?" → "When does it start?"
- 他**什么时候**来？ (Tā **shénme shíhou** lái?) — "He **when** come?" → "When is he coming?"

什么时候 is flexible in position but typically appears before the verb.

## [phase:atomic] Question word 为什么 (wèishénme) - why
tags: question-words, why, reason

The question word **为什么** (wèishénme, "why") asks for reasons or causes. It typically appears at the beginning or after the subject.

**Structure:** 为什么 + statement OR Subject + 为什么 + verb

Examples:
- **为什么**你不去？ (**Wèishénme** nǐ bú qù?) — "**Why** you not go?" → "Why don't you go?"
- 你**为什么**喜欢他？ (Nǐ **wèishénme** xǐhuan tā?) — "You **why** like him?" → "Why do you like him?"
- **为什么**？ (**Wèishénme**?) — "**Why**?"

Answers to 为什么 often start with 因为 (yīnwèi, "because").

## [phase:atomic] Question word 怎么 (zěnme) - how
tags: question-words, how, method

The question word **怎么** (zěnme, "how") asks about method or manner. It appears before the verb.

**Structure:** Subject + 怎么 + verb

Examples:
- 你**怎么**去？ (Nǐ **zěnme** qù?) — "You **how** go?" → "How do you go?"
- 这个字**怎么**写？ (Zhège zì **zěnme** xiě?) — "This character **how** write?" → "How do you write this character?"
- **怎么**做？ (**Zěnme** zuò?) — "**How** do?" → "How to do it?"

怎么 always appears directly before the verb it modifies.

## [phase:atomic] Question word 多少 (duōshao) - how many/much
tags: question-words, quantity, large-numbers

The question word **多少** (duōshao, "how many/how much") asks about quantity. It is used for larger numbers (typically 10+) or when you don't know if the quantity is large or small.

**Structure:** 多少 + (measure word) + noun OR verb + 多少 + noun

Examples:
- 你有**多少**钱？ (Nǐ yǒu **duōshao** qián?) — "You have **how much** money?"
- **多少**人？ (**Duōshao** rén?) — "**How many** people?"
- 这个**多少**钱？ (Zhège **duōshao** qián?) — "This **how much** money?" → "How much is this?"

The measure word is often optional with 多少, especially for 钱 (money) and 人 (people).

## [phase:atomic] Question word 几 (jǐ) - how many (small numbers)
tags: question-words, quantity, small-numbers

The question word **几** (jǐ, "how many") asks about small quantities, typically expecting an answer of 1-10. It must be followed by a measure word.

**Structure:** 几 + measure word + noun

Examples:
- 你有**几个**孩子？ (Nǐ yǒu **jǐ ge** háizi?) — "You have **how many** children?"
- **几点**了？ (**Jǐ diǎn** le?) — "**What time** is it?" (literally "how many o'clock")
- 他**几岁**？ (Tā **jǐ suì**?) — "He **how many years old**?" → "How old is he?"

几 always requires a measure word, unlike 多少.

## [phase:atomic] Question particle 吗 (ma) - yes/no questions
tags: question-words, yes-no-questions, particles

The particle **吗** (ma) is added to the end of statements to turn them into yes/no questions. This is different from question words that replace specific elements.

**Structure:** Statement + 吗？

Examples:
- 你是学生**吗**？ (Nǐ shì xuésheng **ma**?) — "You are student **[question particle]**?" → "Are you a student?"
- 你好**吗**？ (Nǐ hǎo **ma**?) — "You good **[question particle]**?" → "How are you?" / "Are you well?"
- 这是你的**吗**？ (Zhè shì nǐ de **ma**?) — "This is yours **[question particle]**?" → "Is this yours?"

吗 questions expect 是 (yes) or 不是 (no) answers.

## [phase:complex] Comparing 多少 and 几
tags: question-words, quantity, comparison

Both **多少** and **几** ask "how many," but they're used in different contexts:

**几 (jǐ):**
- Expects a small number (1-10)
- **Must** use a measure word
- Example: 你有几个苹果？ (How many apples? — expecting a small number)

**多少 (duōshao):**
- For larger numbers or unknown quantities
- Measure word is optional
- Example: 你有多少钱？ (How much money? — could be any amount)

**Special case:** When asking age:
- 几岁？ for children (How old? — under 10)
- 多少岁？ for adults (How old? — 10+)
- 多大？ polite way for adults (How old?)

## [phase:complex] Question words with 是 structure
tags: question-words, shi-structure, grammar

Question words work with the 是 (shì, "to be") verb in specific patterns:

**Subject + 是 + question word:**
- 你是**谁**？ (Who are you?)
- 这是**什么**？ (What is this?)
- 他是**哪国**人？ (What nationality is he? — lit. "which country person")

**Question word + 是 + noun:**
- **谁**是老师？ (Who is the teacher?)
- **什么**是爱？ (What is love?)

**Special pattern with 为什么:**
- 你**为什么**是学生？ (Why are you a student?)

Notice that 是 stays in its normal position; only the questioned element is replaced with a question word.

## [phase:complex] Combining time and location questions
tags: question-words, time, location, sentence-structure

When sentences include both time (什么时候) and location (哪里/哪儿) questions, they follow specific word order:

**Basic pattern:** Subject + 什么时候 + 在哪里 + verb

Examples:
- 你**什么时候**去**哪里**？ (When are you going where?) — rare, usually split into two questions
- 你**什么时候**在**哪里**？ (When are you where?) — "When/where are you?"

**More natural:** Ask separately:
- 你**什么时候**去？ (When are you going?)
- 你去**哪里**？ (Where are you going?)

Time information typically comes before location in Chinese: Time + Location + Action.

## [phase:complex] Negative questions with 为什么
tags: question-words, why, negation

**为什么** questions can include negation (不/没) to ask "why not" or "why didn't":

**为什么 + 不 (bù):** Asks why someone doesn't/won't do something (habitual or future)
- 你**为什么不**去？ (Why **don't** you go?)
- **为什么不**喜欢？ (Why **don't** [you] like it?)

**为什么 + 没(有) (méi/méiyǒu):** Asks why someone didn't do something (past)
- 你**为什么没**来？ (Why **didn't** you come?)
- **为什么没有**人？ (Why **aren't there** people? / Why **is there no one**?)

The negative appears immediately after 为什么, before the verb.

## [phase:complex] How questions with manner and method
tags: question-words, how, manner-method

**怎么** (zěnme, "how") asks about method, but related words express different aspects:

**怎么 + verb:** How to do something (method)
- 怎么去？ (How to go? — by what means)
- 怎么做？ (How to do/make?)

**怎么样 (zěnmeyàng):** How about / What about (HSK 2, but useful to distinguish)
- 你怎么样？ (How are you?)
- 这个怎么样？ (How about this one?)

**怎么 in rhetorical questions:**
- 怎么可能？ (How is it possible? — expressing disbelief)

**Position:** 怎么 always appears directly before the verb it modifies.

## [phase:complex] Question words as indefinites
tags: question-words, indefinites, advanced-usage

Question words can function as indefinites when used with 都 (dōu, "all") or in certain patterns:

**Question word + 都 + verb:** "everyone/everything"
- **谁都**喜欢。 (**Everyone** likes it.)
- **什么都**吃。 (Eat **everything** / eat **anything**.)
- **哪儿都**去。 (Go **everywhere** / go **anywhere**.)

**Question word + 都 + 不/没 + verb:** "no one/nothing"
- **谁都不**去。 (**No one** goes.)
- **什么都没**有。 (Have **nothing**.)

This pattern transforms question words into words meaning "any" or "every/no."

## [phase:integration] Question word selection practice
tags: question-words, application, sentence-completion

Choosing the correct question word depends on what element of the sentence you're questioning and what type of answer you expect.

**Decision tree:**
1. **Identity (thing)** → 什么
2. **Identity (person)** → 谁
3. **Selection among options** → 哪 + measure word
4. **Location** → 哪里/哪儿
5. **Time** → 什么时候
6. **Reason** → 为什么
7. **Method** → 怎么
8. **Large/unknown quantity** → 多少
9. **Small quantity (1-10)** → 几 + measure word
10. **Yes/no question** → add 吗

Consider the expected answer to choose correctly.

## [phase:integration] Question formation from statements
tags: question-words, transformation, synthesis

Given a statement, you can form questions by replacing different elements with appropriate question words:

**Statement:** 他明天在学校吃午饭。 (He eats lunch at school tomorrow.)

Possible questions:
- **谁**明天在学校吃午饭？ (Who eats lunch at school tomorrow?)
- 他**什么时候**在学校吃午饭？ (When does he eat lunch at school?)
- 他明天在**哪里**吃午饭？ (Where does he eat lunch tomorrow?)
- 他明天在学校吃**什么**？ (What does he eat at school tomorrow?)
- 他明天在学校**怎么**吃午饭？ (How does he eat lunch at school tomorrow?) — less common

Each question word replaces the element being questioned while keeping the rest of the sentence structure intact.

Start with this statement: 我明天跟朋友去北京。 (I'm going to Beijing with a friend tomorrow.)

Create a question asking WHO is going, using proper word order.

answer: • Uses 谁 (who) as subject to replace 我 • Maintains word order: 谁 + 明天 + 跟朋友 + 去北京 • Correct form: 谁明天跟朋友去北京？ • Note: Placing 明天 before 谁 (明天谁跟朋友去北京) shifts emphasis to time rather than the subject; less natural when 谁 is the primary focus

## [phase:integration] Real-world question scenarios
tags: question-words, practical, communication

Different situations require different question words. Consider context and cultural appropriateness:

**Meeting someone new:**
- 你叫什么名字？ (What's your name?)
- 你是哪国人？ (What's your nationality?)
- 你做什么工作？ (What work do you do?)

**Shopping:**
- 这是什么？ (What is this?)
- 多少钱？ (How much?)
- 有几个？ (How many are there?)

**Making plans:**
- 你什么时候有时间？ (When do you have time?)
- 我们在哪里见？ (Where shall we meet?)
- 怎么去？ (How do we get there?)

**Cultural note:** Some questions (like age or salary) may be considered too direct in Western contexts but are more acceptable in Chinese culture, especially among acquaintances.

### question multiChoice difficulty:3
tags: phase:integration, practical, cultural-context

You're shopping and want to know if they have your size. Which questions are appropriate?

a: 有我的吗？ (Do you have mine?)
b: 有几个？ (How many do you have?)
c: 有哪个号？ (Which sizes do you have?)
d: 为什么没有？ (Why don't you have any?)

answer: ac
explanation: Options A and C appropriately ask about availability. Option B asks quantity, which isn't relevant to finding your size. Option D assumes they don't have any before confirming, which could be rude.

You arranged to meet a friend, but you forgot the time and place. Write TWO questions in Mandarin to ask both pieces of information. Use appropriate question words.

answer: • First question asks WHEN using 什么时候 • Second question asks WHERE using 哪里/哪儿 • Both questions include 我们 (we) and 见面/见 (meet) • Proper sentence structure maintained • Preferred: Two separate questions — 我们什么时候见面？我们在哪里见面？ • Alternative accepted: Combined form 我们什么时候在哪里见？ though less common • Both questions must be present and grammatically correct
