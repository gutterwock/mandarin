## [phase:atomic] Basic meaning of 在
tags: grammar, location, basics

The character 在 (zài) means "to be at" or "to be in/on" a location. It indicates where someone or something is physically located.

When stating location in Chinese, 在 follows this pattern:
**Subject + 在 + Place**

Examples:
- 我在家。(Wǒ zài jiā.) — I am at home.
- 他在学校。(Tā zài xuéxiào.) — He is at school.
- 妈妈在北京。(Māma zài Běijīng.) — Mom is in Beijing.

## [phase:atomic] 在 with common places
tags: vocabulary, places

在 commonly appears with place words to indicate location. Common place expressions include:

- 在家 (zài jiā) — at home
- 在学校 (zài xuéxiào) — at school
- 在医院 (zài yīyuàn) — at the hospital
- 在公司 (zài gōngsī) — at the company/office
- 在北京 (zài Běijīng) — in Beijing
- 在中国 (zài Zhōngguó) — in China
- 在这里 (zài zhèlǐ) — here
- 在那里 (zài nàlǐ) — there

The place word always follows 在 directly.

## [phase:atomic] Asking "where" with 在哪里
tags: questions, location

To ask where someone or something is, use 哪里 (nǎlǐ) or 哪儿 (nǎr) meaning "where" after 在:

**Subject + 在 + 哪里/哪儿？**

Examples:
- 你在哪里？(Nǐ zài nǎlǐ?) — Where are you?
- 他在哪儿？(Tā zài nǎr?) — Where is he?
- 书在哪里？(Shū zài nǎlǐ?) — Where is the book?

The answer follows the same pattern:
- 我在学校。(Wǒ zài xuéxiào.) — I'm at school.

## [phase:atomic] 在 with objects on surfaces
tags: prepositions, surfaces

When describing objects located on surfaces, 在 is used with position words:

- 在桌子上 (zài zhuōzi shàng) — on the table
- 在椅子上 (zài yǐzi shàng) — on the chair
- 在床上 (zài chuáng shàng) — on the bed

The pattern is: **Subject + 在 + Object + 上**

上 (shàng) means "on" or "above."

Example: 书在桌子上。(Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.) — The book is on the table.

## [phase:atomic] 在 with inside/outside locations
tags: prepositions, position

在 combines with other position words to indicate different spatial relationships:

- 在...里 (zài...lǐ) — inside
- 在...外 (zài...wài) — outside
- 在...下 (zài...xià) — under/below

Examples:
- 猫在房间里。(Māo zài fángjiān lǐ.) — The cat is inside the room.
- 狗在房子外面。(Gǒu zài fángzi wàimiàn.) — The dog is outside the house.
- 书在桌子下。(Shū zài zhuōzi xià.) — The book is under the table.

## [phase:atomic] Negative form with 不在
tags: negation, grammar

To say someone or something is NOT at a location, add 不 (bù) before 在:

**Subject + 不在 + Place**

Examples:
- 我不在家。(Wǒ bú zài jiā.) — I'm not at home.
- 他不在学校。(Tā bú zài xuéxiào.) — He's not at school.
- 书不在桌子上。(Shū bú zài zhuōzi shàng.) — The book is not on the table.

Note: 不 changes tone to second tone (bú) before another fourth tone.

## [phase:atomic] 在 vs 有: Basic distinction
tags: comparison, grammar

在 and 有 are both used with locations, but they serve different purposes:

**在** indicates WHERE something/someone is located:
- 我在学校。(I am at school.) — states my location

**有** indicates WHAT EXISTS at a location:
- 学校有学生。(There are students at school.) — states what exists there

Key difference: 在 focuses on the subject's location; 有 focuses on what exists at a place.

## [phase:complex] Using 在 in longer sentences
tags: sentence-patterns, context

在 can appear in longer sentences with additional information. The location phrase with 在 typically comes before the main action:

**Subject + 在 + Place + Verb + Object**

Examples:
- 我在家吃饭。(Wǒ zài jiā chī fàn.) — I eat at home.
- 他在学校学习。(Tā zài xuéxiào xuéxí.) — He studies at school.
- 妈妈在医院工作。(Māma zài yīyuàn gōngzuò.) — Mom works at the hospital.

The 在 phrase tells WHERE the action happens.

## [phase:complex] Distinguishing 在 and 有 in context
tags: comparison, usage

Understanding when to use 在 versus 有 requires attention to the sentence focus:

Use **在** when:
- Stating where a specific person/thing is
- The subject is known and you're locating it
- Pattern: [Known thing] 在 [place]
- Example: 我的书在桌子上。(My book is on the table.)

Use **有** when:
- Stating what exists at a location
- The place is the topic, you're listing what's there
- Pattern: [Place] 有 [thing]
- Example: 桌子上有书。(There is a book on the table.)

## [phase:complex] Complex location descriptions

Multiple position words can be combined with 在 for precise location descriptions:

- 在桌子上面 (zài zhuōzi shàngmiàn) — on top of the table

- 在公司后面 (zài gōngsī hòumiàn) — behind the company

These longer position phrases follow the same 在 pattern.

## [phase:complex] Questions with 在 and specific locations
tags: questions, conversation

When asking about specific aspects of location, 在 can combine with other question words:

- 你在哪个学校？(Nǐ zài nǎge xuéxiào?) — Which school are you at?
- 他在谁的家？(Tā zài shéi de jiā?) — Whose home is he at?
- 书在什么地方？(Shū zài shénme dìfang?) — What place is the book at?

These questions are more specific than just 在哪里？

## [phase:complex] 在 with specific time and location contexts
tags: comparison, usage

At HSK1 level, 在 primarily indicates location. While 在 can theoretically indicate time in advanced Chinese, time expressions typically do not use 在 in standard modern Chinese. Focus on 在 for location contexts:

**Location** (with place words):
- 我在家。(I'm at home.)
- 他在北京。(He's in Beijing.)
- 我在学校。(I'm at school.)

The word that follows 在 determines its meaning — place words clearly indicate location.
