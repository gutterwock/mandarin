## [phase:atomic] Tone Self-Assessment

tags: phonetics, self-evaluation
meta.visual: tone_pair_table

The four tones in Mandarin are the most common stumbling block for HSK 1 learners. Self-assess your tone recognition by testing yourself on minimal pairs—words that differ only in tone.

**Critical tone pairs to test:**
- **1st vs 4th:** mā (妈 mother) vs mà (骂 scold)
- **2nd vs 3rd:** má (麻 numb) vs mǎ (马 horse)
- **2nd vs 4th:** lái (来 come) vs lài (赖 rely on)
- **3rd vs 4th:** hǎo (好 good) vs hào (号 number)

**Self-assessment method:** Listen to each pair without looking at pinyin. Can you identify which tone you heard? If you score below 80% on any pair, that tone contrast needs focused practice.

## [phase:atomic] Vocabulary Gap Identification

tags: vocabulary, self-evaluation

HSK 1 requires recognition of 150 words. Identify gaps by sorting vocabulary into three categories:

**Instant recognition (< 1 second):** You see 我 and immediately think "wǒ, I/me"
**Delayed recognition (2-5 seconds):** You see 喜欢 and need a moment to recall "xǐhuan, to like"
**No recognition:** You see the character but cannot recall meaning or pronunciation

**Action plan based on gaps:**
- **80%+ instant recognition:** You're exam-ready for vocabulary
- **60-79% instant recognition:** Review delayed-recognition words daily
- **Below 60%:** Systematic review of all 150 words needed

Track your category counts weekly to measure progress.

## [phase:atomic] Grammar Pattern Self-Check

tags: grammar, self-evaluation

HSK 1 tests specific grammar patterns. Create a self-assessment checklist:

**Core patterns to verify:**
- **Basic word order:** Subject + Verb + Object (我喝水)
- **Yes/no questions with 吗:** 你好吗?
- **Negation with 不 and 没:** 我不是，我没有
- **Possession with 的:** 我的书
- **Measure words:** 一个人，两本书
- **的 modification:** 好的老师

**Testing method:** For each pattern, write 2-3 original sentences. Check against grammar references. If you make errors or feel uncertain, that pattern needs review.

## [phase:atomic] Character Recognition Speed Test

tags: characters, self-evaluation

HSK 1 reading sections are timed. Beyond knowing characters, you need recognition speed. 

**Self-test protocol:**
Set a timer for 30 seconds. How many HSK 1 characters can you read (character + pinyin + meaning) from a randomized list?

**Benchmark speeds:**
- **15+ characters:** Exam-ready speed
- **10-14 characters:** Adequate but practice needed
- **Below 10:** Recognition is too slow; risk running out of time

**Common slow-down causes:**
- Similar-looking characters (人/入, 大/太, 木/本)
- Characters with multiple meanings (会, 好, 看)
- Infrequent words in your practice materials

## [phase:atomic] Listening Section Skills Audit

tags: listening, self-evaluation

The HSK 1 listening section has specific question types. Assess your performance on each:

**Question Type 1:** Match spoken sentence to picture (6 questions)
**Question Type 2:** Determine if statement is true or false (10 questions)  
**Question Type 3:** Identify correct response to a question (10 questions)
**Question Type 4:** Match dialogue to picture (5 questions)

**Self-assessment approach:** Take sample questions from each type. Calculate your accuracy rate per type, not just overall score.

**Example:** If you score 90% on Type 1 but 60% on Type 3, you need dialogue comprehension practice, not more picture matching.

## [phase:complex] Diagnostic Practice Test Strategy

tags: test-strategy, comprehensive-assessment

A full-length diagnostic test reveals patterns invisible in isolated practice. Take one complete HSK 1 practice test under exam conditions before focused review.

**Exam conditions:**
- Listening: ~30 questions, ~15 minutes (no pausing/repeating audio)
- Reading: 20 questions, 17 minutes (no dictionary)
- No breaks between sections

**Analysis after the test:**
1. **Calculate section scores** (Listening: 100 points, Reading: 100 points, Total: 200 points)
2. **Identify error clusters:** Do mistakes concentrate in certain question numbers (often later questions when fatigued)?
3. **Analyze error types:** Vocabulary unknowns vs. tone confusion vs. grammar mistakes vs. time pressure errors
4. **Review timing:** Did you rush at the end or leave questions blank?

**Passing benchmark:** HSK 1 requires 120/200 points (60%). Most test-takers aim for 150+ (75%) for comfortable passing.

## [phase:complex] Error Pattern Analysis

tags: self-evaluation, test-strategy

Raw scores don't reveal why you make errors. Analyze patterns in your diagnostic test mistakes:

**Vocabulary-based errors:**
- Unknown words you can identify: Create a focused word list
- Known words you forgot: These return with brief review

**Tone/sound-based errors (listening only):**
- Confusing similar syllables (lan/nan, jian/zhan)
- Missing tone distinctions in fast speech

**Grammar-based errors:**
- Incorrect measure word selection
- Word order mistakes
- Misunderstanding question particles (吗, 呢, 吧)

**Time-pressure errors:**
- Mistakes concentrated in final questions
- Rushed guesses without full comprehension

**Pattern recognition:** If 6 out of 8 errors involve the same grammar pattern, that's a learnable gap. If errors are random across all areas, you need broader review.

## [phase:complex] Time Management Self-Assessment

tags: test-strategy, time-management

Many students know the content but fail due to poor time management. Assess your pacing:

**Listening section analysis:**
- Audio controls the pace; no time management decisions needed
- However: Are you still processing question N when question N+1 plays?
- **Solution:** Practice "letting go" of uncertain answers immediately

**Reading section timing:**
- 20 questions in 17 minutes = ~50 seconds per question
- **Fast questions** (Types 1-2, picture matching): 30-40 seconds each
- **Slow questions** (Type 3, sentence completion): 60-70 seconds each
- **Banking time strategy:** Finish Types 1-2 quickly to allow more time for Type 3

**Self-test:** Time yourself on 5 questions of each type. If you exceed target times, you need speed-building practice, not just accuracy work.

## [phase:integration] Mock Exam Day Simulation

tags: test-strategy, exam-preparation, comprehensive

One week before your actual exam, simulate complete exam day conditions:

**Setup:**
- Find a quiet location (library, empty room)
- Bring only allowed materials: pencil, eraser, ID
- No phone, no dictionary, no notes
- Set timer for exact exam duration

**Full simulation sequence:**
1. **Registration/waiting simulation:** Arrive 10 minutes early, sit quietly
2. **Listening section:** 35 questions, ~15 minutes (use practice test audio, no pausing)
3. **5-minute break:** Stand, stretch, bathroom if needed
4. **Reading section:** 20 questions, 17 minutes (strict timing)
5. **Immediate post-test:** Note how you felt about difficulty, timing, stress level

**Simulation benefits:**
- Reveals test-day stamina issues (fatigue in reading section?)
- Tests your actual concentration in timed conditions
- Builds confidence by proving you can complete the full exam
- Identifies any remaining anxiety triggers

**Post-simulation:** Score immediately. This is your most realistic performance predictor.

## [phase:integration] Confidence vs. Competence Calibration

tags: self-evaluation, test-readiness, psychology

Test-readiness requires both competence (actual skill) and confidence (belief in your ability). Assess both:

**Competence indicators:**
- Practice test scores consistently at or above 150/200 (75%)
- Error patterns identified and addressed through targeted review
- Can complete sections within time limits with time to spare
- Second practice test shows improvement over first

**Confidence indicators:**
- Feel calm when starting practice tests
- Can recover mentally after making a mistake
- Sleep well the night before practice tests
- Don't panic when encountering an unknown word or unclear audio

**Four quadrants:**
1. **High competence, high confidence:** Test-ready
2. **High competence, low confidence:** Need confidence-building activities (successful practice, positive self-talk, reviewing what you know well)
3. **Low competence, high confidence:** Need reality check and more focused study
4. **Low competence, low confidence:** Need both skill-building and encouragement

**Calibration exercise:** Before your mock exam, predict your score. After taking it, compare prediction to actual score. Close match (within 10 points) = well-calibrated. Large gap = miscalibration.
