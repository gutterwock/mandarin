## [phase:atomic] The colour red (红 hóng)
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, colours

红 (hóng) means "red" in Chinese. It's one of the most culturally significant colours in Chinese culture, often associated with good fortune and celebration.

Pronunciation: hóng (second tone)
Character breakdown: 红 consists of the silk radical 纟and 工. Note: This is the simplified Chinese form. The traditional character 紅 uses the 糸 (silk) radical instead.

Examples:
- 红色 (hóngsè) - the colour red
- 红茶 (hóngchá) - black tea (literally "red tea")

## [phase:atomic] The attributive marker 的 (de)
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, particle

When an adjective modifies a noun in Chinese, you often use the particle 的 (de) between them: adjective + 的 + noun.

的 - de (neutral tone)

Pattern: [adjective] + 的 + [noun]

Examples:
- 红的苹果 (hóng de píngguǒ) - red apple
- 大的狗 (dà de gǒu) - big dog
- 高的人 (gāo de rén) - tall person

Note: For single-syllable adjectives describing inherent qualities, 的 is sometimes omitted (e.g., 大狗), but using 的 is always safe.

## [phase:complex] Describing objects with colours

## [phase:complex] Distinguishing height vs. length adjectives
tags: phase:complex, application, adjectives

Chinese uses different adjectives for vertical height (高/矮) vs. horizontal/linear length (长/短). Understanding which to use depends on what you're describing.

高 (gāo) / 矮 (ǎi) - for vertical height:
- People's height: 高的人 (tall person)
- Buildings: 高的楼 (tall building)
- Mountains: 高的山 (tall mountain)

长 (cháng) / 短 (duǎn) - for linear length:
- Hair: 长头发 (long hair)
- Roads: 长的路 (long road)
- Objects: 长的桌子 (long table)

## [phase:complex] Body size adjectives usage
tags: phase:complex, application, body

胖 (pàng) and 瘦 (shòu) specifically describe body weight or build. They're different from size adjectives like 大 (big) and 小 (small).

For people and animals:
- 胖的猫 (pàng de māo) - fat cat (body build)
- 大的猫 (dà de māo) - big cat (overall size)
- 瘦的狗 (shòu de gǒu) - thin dog (body build)
- 小的狗 (xiǎo de gǒu) - small dog (overall size)

Note: 胖 and 瘦 are not typically used for inanimate objects. Use 大 and 小 instead.

## [phase:complex] Formal vs. casual colour descriptions
tags: phase:complex, usage, colours

Chinese has both formal and casual ways to describe colours. Understanding when to use each is important for natural speech.

Formal/explicit (using 色):
- 红色的车 (hóngsè de chē) - red car
- 蓝色的天 (lánsè de tiān) - blue sky

Casual (without 色):
- 红的车 (hóng de chē) - red car
- 蓝的天 (lán de tiān) - blue sky

In writing or formal contexts, 色 is preferred. In everyday speech, it's often dropped. Both are grammatically correct.
