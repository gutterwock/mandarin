## [phase:atomic] Rice and Meal (饭)
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, food, noun

**饭** (fàn) means both "rice" (cooked rice) and "meal" in general.

- **Pronunciation:** fàn (falling tone, 4th tone)
- **Character breakdown:** 饣(food radical) + 反
- **Common usage:** 吃饭 (chīfàn) = eat a meal/eat rice

When someone asks 你吃饭了吗？(Nǐ chīfàn le ma?), they're asking "Have you eaten?" — a common Chinese greeting.

## [phase:atomic] Noodles (面条)
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, food, noun

**面条** (miàntiáo) means "noodles."

- **Pronunciation:** miàntiáo (4th tone + 2nd tone)
- **Character breakdown:** 面 (noodles/flour/dough) + 条 (strip/long thin piece). In modern usage, 面 more commonly means 'noodles' or 'flour/dough' rather than just 'wheat'.

Noodles are a staple food throughout China, with regional variations in preparation and style.

## [phase:atomic] Meat (肉)
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, food, noun

**肉** (ròu) means "meat."

- **Character note:** Single-character word; the radical 月 originally represented meat/flesh. In the character 肉 itself, the bottom component derives from the meat radical, which looks similar to the moon radical 月 in modern form but has a different origin — a common point of confusion.

Meat is typically an important part of Chinese meals, though the type varies by region.

## [phase:atomic] Please Give Me (请给我)
tags: phase:atomic, vocabulary, phrase, politeness

**请给我** (qǐng gěi wǒ) means "please give me."

- **Pronunciation:** qǐng gěi wǒ (all three syllables are 3rd tone, but due to tone sandhi rules, 请 and 给 become 2nd tone when spoken: qíng géi wǒ)
- **Breakdown:** 请 (please) + 给 (give) + 我 (me)
- **Usage:** Polite way to request something: 请给我一杯水 (Qǐng gěi wǒ yì bēi shuǐ) = Please give me a glass of water
- **More polite than:** Just using 我要... (I want...)

This phrase is especially useful in restaurants and shops.

## [phase:complex] Ordering Multiple Items
tags: phase:complex, conversation, ordering, restaurant

When ordering multiple items, you can list them with 要 or use 和 (and):

**Pattern 1:** 我要 + item 1 + 和 + item 2
- Example: 我要一碗面条和一杯茶 (I want a bowl of noodles and a cup of tea)

**Pattern 2:** Separate sentences
- 我要一碗面条。我也要一杯茶。(I want a bowl of noodles. I also want a cup of tea.)

**Adding politeness:** Start with 请给我...
- 请给我一碗面条和一杯水 (Please give me a bowl of noodles and a glass of water)

## [phase:complex] Measure Words with Food
tags: phase:complex, grammar, measure-words

Different foods use different measure words:

**碗 (wǎn) - bowl:** 一碗饭 (a bowl of rice), 一碗面条 (a bowl of noodles)

**杯 (bēi) - cup/glass:** 一杯水 (a glass of water), 一杯茶 (a cup of tea), 一杯咖啡 (a cup of coffee)

**个 (gè) - general:** 一个苹果 (an apple), 一个鸡蛋 (an egg)

**瓶 (píng) - bottle:** 一瓶啤酒 (a bottle of beer), 一瓶水 (a bottle of water)

The measure word often relates to how the food is served or its natural container.

## [phase:complex] Restaurant Interaction Sequence
tags: phase:complex, conversation, pragmatics, restaurant

A typical restaurant ordering sequence:

1. **Server greets:** 你好！(Hello!)
2. **You indicate readiness:** 我要... (I want...) or 请给我... (Please give me...)
3. **You order items:** State food and drinks with measure words
4. **Server may ask:** 还要吗？(Anything else?) or 还要别的吗？(Note: 别的 is HSK2 vocabulary; 还要吗？is simpler)
5. **You finish:** 不要了，谢谢 (No more, thank you) or add more items

**Complete example:**
- Server: 你好！
- You: 请给我一碗面条和一杯茶。
- Server: 好的。还要吗？
- You: 不要了，谢谢。

## [phase:complex] Compound Food Words
tags: phase:complex, vocabulary, word-formation

Many Chinese food words are compounds showing logical structure:

**With 果 (fruit):**
- 水果 (water + fruit = fruit in general)
- 苹果 (phonetic + fruit = apple)

**With origin + product:**
- 鸡蛋 (chicken + egg = chicken egg)
- 牛奶 (cow + milk = cow's milk)

**Phonetic loanwords:**
- 咖啡 (kāfēi = coffee, phonetic)
- 啤酒 (pí + jiǔ = beer, 啤 is phonetic, 酒 means alcohol)

Understanding these patterns helps you remember vocabulary and guess meanings of new food words.

## [phase:integration] Cross-Cultural Food Discussion
tags: phase:integration, culture, discussion, food-culture

Chinese meal structure and ordering customs differ from Western practices:

**Differences:**
- Chinese meals typically include a grain base (饭 or 面条) with dishes, not separate courses
- Tea or water is standard; coffee is less traditional
- Food is often shared family-style rather than individual portions
- Asking 好吃吗？ is a common way to show interest in others' meals

**Similarities:**
- Both cultures value fresh ingredients
- Meals are social occasions
- Complimenting food is polite

**In restaurants:** Chinese restaurants may bring all dishes at once, and it's normal to order tea before the meal arrives.

## [phase:integration] Creating a Balanced Order
tags: phase:integration, application, authentic-task, nutrition

Using HSK 1 vocabulary, create a balanced restaurant order:

**Consider including:**
- A staple (饭 or 面条)
- Protein (肉 or 鸡蛋)
- Vegetables/fruit (菜, 水果, or 苹果)
- A beverage (水, 茶, 咖啡, 牛奶, or 啤酒)

**Structure your order:**
- Use proper measure words
- Connect items logically
- Use polite forms (请给我...)

**Example pattern:** 请给我 + [staple] + 和 + [protein/vegetable] + 和 + [beverage]。

## [phase:integration] Situational Communication
tags: phase:integration, conversation, problem-solving, authentic-task

**Situation:** You're at a restaurant. The server brings you coffee, but you ordered tea. You need to:
1. Politely indicate the mistake
2. Restate what you want

**Useful structures:**
- 这是咖啡 (This is coffee)
- 我要茶 (I want tea)
- 请给我茶 (Please give me tea)

In Chinese culture, politely pointing out errors is acceptable. You don't need to apologize excessively.
