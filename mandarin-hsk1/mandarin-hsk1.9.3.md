## [phase:atomic] 工作 (gōngzuò) — Work/Job
tags: vocabulary, nouns, work

The word **工作** (gōngzuò) means both "work" and "job" in Mandarin. It can function as a noun or a verb.

As a noun: 我有工作 (wǒ yǒu gōngzuò) — I have a job.

As a verb: 我工作 (wǒ gōngzuò) — I work.

The character 工 originally depicted a carpenter's square, symbolizing labor and work.

## [phase:atomic] 学习 (xuéxí) — Study/Learn
tags: vocabulary, verbs, education

**学习** (xuéxí) means "to study" or "to learn." It's a common verb used for educational activities.

Examples:
- 我学习汉语 (wǒ xuéxí hànyǔ) — I study Chinese.
- 他学习英语 (tā xuéxí yīngyǔ) — He studies English.

The first character 学 means "learn" and the second 习 means "practice."

## [phase:atomic] 汉语 (hànyǔ) — Chinese Language
tags: vocabulary, nouns, languages

**汉语** (hànyǔ) specifically refers to the Chinese language, particularly Mandarin. 汉 (hàn) refers to the Han ethnicity (the majority ethnic group in China), and 语 (yǔ) means "language."

Example: 我学习汉语 (wǒ xuéxí hànyǔ) — I study Chinese.

This is the standard term used in mainland China for the Chinese language.

## [phase:atomic] 老师 (lǎoshī) — Teacher
tags: vocabulary, nouns, professions

**老师** (lǎoshī) means "teacher." It's a respectful term used for educators at all levels. 老 (lǎo) means "old" (conveying respect), and 师 (shī) means "master" or "teacher."

You can address teachers directly as 老师 without using their name: 老师好！ (lǎoshī hǎo!) — Hello, teacher!

## [phase:atomic] 电话 (diànhuà) — Telephone/Phone Call
tags: vocabulary, nouns, technology

**电话** (diànhuà) means "telephone" or "phone call." The character 电 means "electricity" and 话 means "speech" or "words," so it literally means "electric speech."

It can refer to the device or the action of calling:
- 我有电话 (wǒ yǒu diànhuà) — I have a phone.
- 打电话 (dǎ diànhuà) — to make a phone call (a common collocation introduced for recognition; 打 will be taught in future levels).

## [phase:atomic] 问题 (wèntí) — Question/Problem

## [phase:complex] Work and Study Contexts
tags: grammar, usage, context

When discussing work and study, certain word patterns are common:

**Identity statements with 是:**
- 我是学生 (wǒ shì xuésheng) — I am a student
- 她是老师 (tā shì lǎoshī) — She is a teacher
- 他是医生 (tā shì yīshēng) — He is a doctor

**Activity statements (no 是):**
- 我学习汉语 (wǒ xuéxí hànyǔ) — I study Chinese
- 他工作 (tā gōngzuò) — He works

**Possession with 有:**
- 我有电脑 (wǒ yǒu diànnǎo) — I have a computer
- 你有书吗？(nǐ yǒu shū ma?) — Do you have a book?

## [phase:complex] Technology and Communication
tags: vocabulary, thematic-sets, technology

The "electric" (电) words form a common pattern in Mandarin for modern technology:

- **电话** (diànhuà) — telephone (electric + speech)
- **电脑** (diànnǎo) — computer (electric + brain)

Both words share 电 (electricity) and combine it with traditional concepts:
- 话 (huà) = speech/words
- 脑 (nǎo) = brain

This pattern helps you remember and understand other technology words you'll encounter later.

## [phase:integration] Cross-Cultural Professional Context
tags: integration, real-world, culture

In Chinese professional settings, addressing people correctly matters:

- Teachers are often called 老师 even without their surname
- Doctors use their surname + 医生: 王医生 (Dr. Wang)
- Introducing yourself includes your profession: 我是... + profession
- Asking about someone's work: 你工作吗？ is a simple yes/no question. Asking about someone's profession requires different structures (preview for future levels: 你做什么工作？)

These conventions apply whether discussing 工作 (work), 学习 (study), or daily activities.
