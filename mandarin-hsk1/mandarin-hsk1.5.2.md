## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 个 (gè) - General Classifier
tags: measure-words, 个, general-classifier

个 (gè) is the most common measure word in Mandarin Chinese. It's used with people and many general objects when you don't know or can't remember a more specific measure word.

**Structure:** Number + 个 + Noun

Examples:
- 一个人 (yī gè rén) - one person
- 两个朋友 (liǎng gè péngyou) - two friends
- 三个学生 (sān gè xuésheng) - three students

When counting objects like apples, chairs, or rooms, 个 is often appropriate:
- 一个苹果 (yī gè píngguǒ) - one apple
- 五个房间 (wǔ gè fángjiān) - five rooms

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 本 (běn) - Books and Bound Items
tags: measure-words, 本, books

本 (běn) is the specific measure word used for books, magazines, notebooks, and other bound printed materials.

**Structure:** Number + 本 + Book-related Noun

Examples:
- 一本书 (yī běn shū) - one book
- 两本杂志 (liǎng běn zázhì) - two magazines
- 三本词典 (sān běn cídiǎn) - three dictionaries

This measure word comes from the idea of a "volume" or "edition."

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 杯 (bēi) - Cups and Glasses
tags: measure-words, 杯, beverages

杯 (bēi) is used for beverages served in cups or glasses. The word itself means "cup" or "glass."

**Structure:** Number + 杯 + Beverage

Examples:
- 一杯水 (yī bēi shuǐ) - one glass of water
- 两杯茶 (liǎng bēi chá) - two cups of tea
- 三杯咖啡 (sān bēi kāfēi) - three cups of coffee

This measure word indicates both the container and the quantity.

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 块 (kuài) - Pieces and Money
tags: measure-words, 块, money-pieces

块 (kuài) has two main uses: for pieces or chunks of something, and as the standard colloquial word for yuan (Chinese currency). 元 (yuán) is the formal/written form, but 块 is what you'll hear in everyday conversation.

**Structure:** Number + 块 + Noun (or just 块 for money)

Examples for pieces:
- 一块蛋糕 (yī kuài dàngāo) - one piece of cake
- 两块肉 (liǎng kuài ròu) - two pieces of meat

Examples for money:
- 三块钱 (sān kuài qián) - three yuan
- 五块 (wǔ kuài) - five yuan (钱 can be omitted)

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 只 (zhī) - Small Animals
tags: measure-words, 只, animals

只 (zhī) is used for small animals, particularly birds, cats, dogs, and other pets or small creatures. At HSK 1 level, 只 is the standard measure word for the common animals you'll encounter in daily conversation.

**Structure:** Number + 只 + Animal

Examples:
- 一只猫 (yī zhī māo) - one cat
- 两只狗 (liǎng zhī gǒu) - two dogs
- 三只鸟 (sān zhī niǎo) - three birds

Note: Other measure words like 匹 (pǐ) for horses and 头 (tóu) for larger animals exist, but are not required at the HSK 1 level.

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 条 (tiáo) - Long Thin Objects

## [phase:atomic] Measure Word 件 (jiàn) - Clothing and Matters

## [phase:atomic] Question Word 几 (jǐ) - How Many (Small Numbers)
tags: question-words, 几, how-many

几 (jǐ) means "how many" and is used when asking about quantities, typically when expecting a small number (usually under 10) as an answer.

**Structure:** 几 + Measure Word + Noun

Examples:
- 几个人? (jǐ gè rén?) - How many people?
- 几本书? (jǐ běn shū?) - How many books?
- 几杯茶? (jǐ bēi chá?) - How many cups of tea?

几 implies the speaker expects a specific, countable, relatively small number.

## [phase:atomic] Question Word 多少 (duōshao) - How Many/Much (Larger Quantities)
tags: question-words, 多少, how-many

多少 (duōshao) means "how many" or "how much" and is used for larger quantities or when you're unsure of the magnitude. It can be used with or without measure words.

**Structure:** 多少 + (Measure Word) + Noun

Examples:
- 多少钱? (duōshao qián?) - How much money?
- 多少人? (duōshao rén?) - How many people? (large group)
- 多少个学生? (duōshao gè xuésheng?) - How many students?

多少 is more flexible than 几 and can be used for any quantity.

## [phase:atomic] Using 多少 Without Measure Words
tags: question-words, 多少, flexibility

Unlike 几, which always requires a measure word, 多少 can be used directly before certain nouns, especially when asking about uncountable quantities or prices.

Examples:
- 多少钱? (duōshao qián?) - How much money? (no measure word needed)
- 多少人? (duōshao rén?) - How many people? (measure word optional)

Compare:
- 几个人? (jǐ gè rén?) - requires 个
- 多少个人? (duōshao gè rén?) - optional 个
- 多少人? (duōshao rén?) - no measure word needed

## [phase:complex] Choosing Appropriate Measure Words
tags: measure-words, selection, context

Selecting the correct measure word requires understanding the category and characteristics of the noun. Consider these factors:

**Physical characteristics:**
- Flat? → 张 (paper, tables, beds)
- Long and thin? → 条 (fish, rivers, pants)
- Bound volumes? → 本 (books, magazines)

**Category:**
- People or general objects? → 个
- Small animals? → 只
- Clothing (upper body)? → 件
- Beverages? → 杯

**Context:**
- Money? → 块
- Abstract matters? → 件

When uncertain, 个 is often acceptable as a general classifier, though using the specific measure word is more natural and correct.

## [phase:complex] Numbers with Measure Words
tags: measure-words, numbers, structure

When combining numbers with measure words, follow this pattern:

**Structure:** Number + Measure Word + Noun

Important points:
- 二 (èr) vs. 两 (liǎng): Use 两 (not 二) with measure words
  - Correct: 两个人 (liǎng gè rén)
  - Incorrect: 二个人 (èr gè rén)
- This applies to all measure words: 两本书, 两杯茶, 两张桌子
- Note: While 个 is a general classifier that can be used when the specific measure word is forgotten, the correct measure word is always preferred. For example, 两只猫 (with 只) is more natural than 两个猫 (with 个).

Numbers 1, 3-10 use their standard forms:
- 一个 (yī gè), 三个 (sān gè), 四个 (sì gè), etc.

## [phase:complex] Question Formation with Measure Words
tags: question-words, measure-words, sentences

When forming questions about quantity, the structure depends on whether you use 几 or 多少:

**With 几 (expecting small numbers):**
- Question: Subject + 有 + 几 + MW + Noun?
- Example: 你有几本书? (Nǐ yǒu jǐ běn shū?) - How many books do you have?

**With 多少 (larger or unknown quantities):**
- Question: Subject + 有 + 多少 + (MW) + Noun?
- Example: 这个多少钱? (Zhège duōshao qián?) - How much is this?

**Answer structure:**
- Subject + 有 + Number + MW + Noun
- Example: 我有三本书 (Wǒ yǒu sān běn shū) - I have three books.

## [phase:complex] Common Measure Word Errors
tags: measure-words, errors, corrections

Common mistakes to avoid:

**Error 1: Omitting the measure word**
- Incorrect: 一书 (yī shū)
- Correct: 一本书 (yī běn shū)

**Error 2: Using the wrong measure word**
- Incorrect: 一个书 (should use 本 for books)
- Correct: 一本书 (yī běn shū)

**Error 3: Using 二 instead of 两 with measure words**
- Incorrect: 二本书 (èr běn shū)
- Correct: 两本书 (liǎng běn shū)

**Error 4: Wrong word order**
- Incorrect: 书一本 (shū yī běn)
- Correct: 一本书 (yī běn shū)

The pattern is always: Number + Measure Word + Noun

## [phase:complex] Beverages and Container Measures
tags: measure-words, 杯, beverages, usage

杯 (bēi) is specifically for beverages in cups or glasses. Understanding when to use it:

**Use 杯 for:**
- 茶 (chá) - tea
- 咖啡 (kāfēi) - coffee
- 水 (shuǐ) - water
- 牛奶 (niúnǎi) - milk
- 果汁 (guǒzhī) - juice

**Structure for ordering:** 我要 + Number + 杯 + Beverage
- 我要一杯茶 (Wǒ yào yī bēi chá) - I want a cup of tea
- 我要两杯咖啡 (Wǒ yào liǎng bēi kāfēi) - I want two cups of coffee

杯 indicates both the container and the serving, so it means "a cup/glass of" rather than just counting empty containers.
