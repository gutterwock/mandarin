## [phase:atomic] 喜欢 (xǐhuan) - To like
tags: vocabulary, verbs, emotions

The verb **喜欢** (xǐhuan) means "to like" and is one of the most common verbs for expressing preferences in Mandarin.

**喜欢** is pronounced xǐhuan, where 喜 (xǐ) is third tone and 欢 (huan) is a light/neutral tone in standard pronunciation: xǐ·huan.

Basic structure:
- Subject + 喜欢 + object (noun or verb)

Examples:
- 我喜欢猫。(Wǒ xǐhuan māo.) — I like cats.
- 他喜欢茶。(Tā xǐhuan chá.) — He likes tea.
- 她喜欢狗。(Tā xǐhuan gǒu.) — She likes dogs.

## [phase:atomic] 喜欢 + Noun: Expressing likes for things
tags: sentence-structure, preferences

When you like a noun (person, thing, place), place the noun directly after **喜欢**:

**Subject + 喜欢 + Noun**

Examples:
- 我喜欢中国菜。(Wǒ xǐhuan Zhōngguó cài.) — I like Chinese food.
- 他喜欢北京。(Tā xǐhuan Běijīng.) — He likes Beijing.
- 她喜欢医生。(Tā xǐhuan yīshēng.) — She likes doctors.
- 我喜欢书。(Wǒ xǐhuan shū.) — I like books.

The noun can be any person, place, thing, or food you have a preference for.

## [phase:atomic] 喜欢 + Verb: Expressing likes for activities
tags: sentence-structure, preferences, verbs

**喜欢** can also be followed by another verb to express that you like doing an activity:

**Subject + 喜欢 + Verb (+ Object)**

Examples:
- 我喜欢看电影。(Wǒ xǐhuan kàn diànyǐng.) — I like watching movies.
- 他喜欢喝茶。(Tā xǐhuan hē chá.) — He likes drinking tea.
- 她喜欢看书。(Tā xǐhuan kàn shū.) — She likes reading books.
- 我喜欢吃中国菜。(Wǒ xǐhuan chī Zhōngguó cài.) — I like eating Chinese food.

The verb after 喜欢 describes the activity you enjoy.

## [phase:atomic] 想 (xiǎng) - To want to / to miss
tags: vocabulary, verbs, desires

The verb **想** (xiǎng) primarily means "to want to" (do something) or "to think/miss."

**想** is pronounced in the third tone (falling-rising): xiǎng.

When followed by a verb, it means "to want to (do something)":
- 我想去。(Wǒ xiǎng qù.) — I want to go.
- 他想喝茶。(Tā xiǎng hē chá.) — He wants to drink tea.

**想** can also mean "to miss" (someone) or "to think about," but at HSK 1 level, focus on the "want to" meaning when followed by verbs.

The verb after 想 describes the action you want to perform.

## [phase:atomic] Difference between 喜欢 and 想
tags: grammar, comparison

**喜欢** and **想** express different concepts:

**喜欢** = "to like" (general preference or enjoyment)
- 我喜欢喝茶。(Wǒ xǐhuan hē chá.) — I like drinking tea. (in general)

**想** = "to want to" (specific desire at a moment)
- 我想喝茶。(Wǒ xiǎng hē chá.) — I want to drink tea. (right now)

Think of it this way:
- 喜欢 describes what you enjoy in general
- 想 describes what you desire at a particular time

You can like something without wanting it right now, and you can want something you don't particularly like.

## [phase:atomic] Negating 喜欢 with 不喜欢
tags: negation, grammar

To say you don't like something, add **不** (bù) before **喜欢**:

**Subject + 不喜欢 + Noun/Verb**

Examples:
- 我不喜欢猫。(Wǒ bù xǐhuan māo.) — I don't like cats.
- 他不喜欢喝咖啡。(Tā bù xǐhuan hē kāfēi.) — He doesn't like drinking coffee.
- 她不喜欢看书。(Tā bù xǐhuan kàn shū.) — She doesn't like reading books.

The structure is exactly the same as the positive form, just add 不 before 喜欢.

## [phase:atomic] 不要 (bùyào) - Don't / Don't want
tags: negation, grammar, comparison

**不要** (bùyào) can mean "don't" (imperative) or "don't want":

As a command ("don't do something"):
- 不要去!(Bùyào qù!) — Don't go!
- 不要喝!(Bùyào hē!) — Don't drink!

As "don't want" (similar to 不想 but stronger or more definite):
- 我不要。(Wǒ bùyào.) — I don't want it.

**不想** = don't want to (softer, less desire)
**不要** = don't want (stronger refusal) or imperative "don't"

At HSK 1 level, recognize that both exist and that 不要 is often used for commands.

## [phase:complex] Comparing 喜欢, 想, and 不要
tags: grammar, nuance, comparison

Understanding when to use each expression:

**喜欢 (general preference):**
- 我喜欢喝咖啡。— I like drinking coffee. (in general)

**想 (current desire):**
- 我想喝咖啡。— I want to drink coffee. (right now)

**不想 (don't want to, lack of desire):**
- 我不想喝咖啡。— I don't want to drink coffee. (at this moment)

**不喜欢 (general dislike):**
- 我不喜欢咖啡。— I don't like coffee. (in general)

**不要 (strong refusal or command):**
- 不要喝咖啡!— Don't drink coffee! (command)
- 我不要咖啡。— I don't want coffee. (refusal, stronger than 不想)

## [phase:complex] Negation patterns: 不 usage
tags: grammar, negation, patterns

The negation word **不** (bù) is used consistently with 喜欢, 想, and 要:

**Pattern: 不 + Verb**

Examples:
- 喜欢 → 不喜欢 (don't like)
- 想 → 不想 (don't want to)
- 要 → 不要 (don't want / don't)

This is the standard negation pattern for most verbs in Mandarin. Simply place 不 directly before the verb.

Note: In spoken Mandarin, 不 automatically changes to a higher tone before fourth tone syllables (like yào in 不要). This is a natural pronunciation pattern that occurs automatically in speech. At HSK 1 level, you don't need to memorize this separately—just listen and imitate native pronunciation.

## [phase:integration] Expressing complex preferences
tags: production, preferences, integration

Combining 喜欢 and 想 to express nuanced preferences:

**Stating both general preferences and current desires:**
- 我喜欢喝茶,但是我现在想喝咖啡。— I like drinking tea, but now I want to drink coffee.
- 他喜欢中国菜,他想吃中国菜。— He likes Chinese food, (and) he wants to eat Chinese food.

**Expressing contrasts:**
- 我不喜欢看书,但是我想看这本书。— I don't like reading books, but I want to read this book. (Note: 这 = this, 本 = measure word for books; these are introduced here as new vocabulary.)

**Multiple preferences:**
- 我喜欢喝茶,也喜欢喝咖啡。— I like drinking tea, and also like drinking coffee.
- 我想去北京,也想去上海。— I want to go to Beijing, and also want to go to Shanghai.

## [phase:integration] Making and declining suggestions
tags: conversation, social-interaction, integration

Using 想 and 喜欢 in suggestions and responses:

**Making suggestions:**
- 你想喝茶吗?— Do you want to drink tea?
- 我们去看电影,好吗?— Let's go watch a movie, okay?

**Accepting:**
- 好!我想去。— Good! I want to go.
- 我很喜欢看电影!— I really like watching movies!

**Declining politely:**
- 对不起,我不想去。— Sorry, I don't want to go.
- 我不喜欢看电影。— I don't like watching movies.
- 我不想喝茶,我想喝咖啡。— I don't want to drink tea, I want to drink coffee.

**Strong refusal (use carefully):**
- 不要!— Don't! / I don't want (to)!

## [phase:integration] Synthesis: Preferences and desires in context
tags: integration, real-world-application, synthesis

Real-world application of 喜欢, 想, and negations:

**At a restaurant:**
- Server: 你想喝什么?
- You: 我不喜欢咖啡。我想喝茶。

**Planning with friends:**
- Friend: 你想去哪儿?
- You: 我想去看电影。你喜欢看电影吗?

**Discussing preferences:**
- New acquaintance: 你喜欢中国菜吗?
- You: 我很喜欢!我也喜欢喝中国茶。

Remember:
- Use **喜欢** for general preferences (things you enjoy)
- Use **想** for specific desires (what you want now/soon)
- Use **不** to negate both
- Use **不要** for strong refusals or commands
- Combine them to express nuanced thoughts

b: Politely declining tea: 不要茶,谢谢。
c: Telling a child not to run: 不要跑!
d: Answering a preference question: 我不要看书。
answer: ac
explanation: 不要 is most appropriate for commands/warnings (A, C). For declining offers (B), both 不要茶,谢谢 and 我不想喝茶,谢谢 are commonly used and understood, though 我不想... or 不用了... are slightly more conversational in tone. For preference questions (D), 我不想看书 or 我不喜欢看书 is more natural.
