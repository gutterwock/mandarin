## [phase:atomic] The basic structure: adjective as predicate
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, sentence-structure

In Mandarin Chinese, adjectives can function directly as the main verb (predicate) of a sentence, without needing a copula verb like "to be." This creates a fundamentally different structure from English.

The basic pattern is: **Subject + Adjective**

Examples:
- 天气好。(Tiānqì hǎo.) — The weather is good.
- 水果贵。(Shuǐguǒ guì.) — The fruit is expensive.
- 他忙。(Tā máng.) — He is busy.

Notice that there is no equivalent of "is/are/am" in these sentences. The adjective directly describes the subject's state or quality.

## [phase:atomic] The role of 很 (hěn) with adjective predicates
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, adverbs

When using adjectives as predicates in neutral, objective statements, Mandarin requires the adverb 很 (hěn) to appear before the adjective. In this context, 很 does NOT emphasize "very" — it functions as a grammatical dummy adverb.

The pattern is: **Subject + 很 + Adjective**

Examples:
- 他很高。(Tā hěn gāo.) — He is tall. (NOT "He is very tall")
- 这个苹果很好吃。(Zhège píngguǒ hěn hǎochī.) — This apple is delicious.
- 教室很大。(Jiàoshì hěn dà.) — The classroom is big.

Without 很, the sentence gains a comparative or contrastive meaning (explained in a later section).

## [phase:atomic] Negating adjective predicates with 不 (bù)
tags: phase:atomic, negation, grammar

To negate adjective predicates, use 不 (bù) directly before the adjective. When 不 is present, you do NOT use 很.

The pattern is: **Subject + 不 + Adjective**

Examples:
- 不贵 (bù guì) — not expensive
- 不好看 (bù hǎokàn) — not good-looking
- 不冷 (bù lěng) — not cold
- 他不高。(Tā bù gāo.) — He is not tall.
- 这个菜不好吃。(Zhège cài bù hǎochī.) — This dish is not delicious.

## [phase:atomic] Bare adjectives imply comparison or contrast
tags: phase:atomic, grammar, pragmatics

When an adjective appears WITHOUT 很 (or another adverb) in an affirmative sentence, it carries a comparative or contrastive meaning. The sentence implies "more X than expected" or "X compared to something/someone else."

Compare:
- 他很高。(Tā hěn gāo.) — He is tall. [neutral statement]
- 他高。(Tā gāo.) — He is tall(er). [implies comparison: taller than expected, taller than average, or taller than someone else]

More examples:
- 这个便宜。(Zhège piányi.) — This one is cheaper. [compared to another]
- 今天冷。(Jīntiān lěng.) — Today is cold(er). [compared to other days or expectation]

## [phase:atomic] Position of 很 in the sentence
tags: phase:atomic, word-order

The adverb 很 must appear immediately before the adjective, between the subject and the predicate adjective.

Correct pattern: **Subject + 很 + Adjective**

Examples:
- 水很热。(Shuǐ hěn rè.) — The water is hot. ✓
- 很水热。— INCORRECT ✗
- 水热很。— INCORRECT ✗

Other adverbs (like 也 "also") come before 很:
- 他也很忙。(Tā yě hěn máng.) — He is also busy.

## [phase:complex] Comparing neutral and comparative statements
tags: phase:complex, grammar, pragmatics

Understanding when to use 很 versus bare adjectives is crucial for natural communication. The presence or absence of 很 changes the pragmatic meaning.

**With 很 (neutral, objective):**
- 他很高。— He is tall. [simple description]
- 这个很好吃。— This is delicious. [straightforward opinion]

**Without 很 (comparative, contrastive):**
- 他高。— He is tall(er). [implies: compared to someone else, taller than expected]
- 这个好吃。— This is delicious(er). [implies: better tasting than another option]

Context often clarifies the comparison:
- A: 这个贵吗？(Is this expensive?) B: 不贵。(It's not expensive.) — neutral negative
- A: 这个怎么样？(How is this one?) B: 这个贵。(This one is expensive(r).) — comparative

## [phase:complex] Using adjective predicates with demonstratives
tags: phase:complex, sentence-structure, demonstratives

Demonstrative pronouns (这 zhè "this", 那 nà "that") and measure words commonly appear as subjects with adjective predicates.

Pattern: **Demonstrative + (Measure Word) + Noun + 很 + Adjective**

Examples:
- 这个很好。(Zhège hěn hǎo.) — This one is good.
- 那个很贵。(Nàge hěn guì.) — That one is expensive.
- 这本书很有意思。(Zhè běn shū hěn yǒuyìsi.) — This book is interesting.
- 那件衣服很漂亮。(Nà jiàn yīfu hěn piàoliang.) — That piece of clothing is pretty.

For simple "this/that" without specifying the noun, use 这个 or 那个:
- 这个不冷。(Zhège bù lěng.) — This (one) is not cold.

## [phase:complex] Combining negation with multiple adjectives
tags: phase:complex, negation, vocabulary

You can negate different adjectives to express various states. Each negation follows the 不 + adjective pattern.

Examples with multiple contexts:
- 不大也不小 (bù dà yě bù xiǎo) — neither big nor small [using 也 "also"]
- 不冷不热 (bù lěng bù rè) — neither cold nor hot
- 他不高,很胖。(Tā bù gāo, hěn pàng.) — He is not tall, (but he is) fat.

When connecting two adjective predicates about the same subject:
- Use commas or conjunctions like 也 (also), 但是 (but)
- 东西不贵,很好。(Dōngxi bù guì, hěn hǎo.) — Things are not expensive, (they're) good.

## [phase:complex] Adjective predicates with time words
tags: phase:complex, time-expressions, sentence-structure

Time expressions typically appear before the subject or between the subject and predicate in sentences with adjective predicates.

Common patterns:
- **Time + Subject + 很 + Adjective**
- **Subject + Time + 很 + Adjective**

Examples:
- 今天很冷。(Jīntiān hěn lěng.) — Today is cold.
- 现在很忙。(Xiànzài hěn máng.) — (I'm) busy now.
- 他今天不忙。(Tā jīntiān bù máng.) — He is not busy today.
- 明天很热。(Míngtiān hěn rè.) — Tomorrow will be hot.

## [phase:complex] Question forms with adjective predicates
tags: phase:complex, questions, grammar

To form yes-no questions with adjective predicates, use the question particle 吗 (ma) at the end, or use the positive-negative question form.

**With 吗:**
- Statement: 很贵。→ Question: 很贵吗?(Is it expensive?)
- Statement: 他很忙。→ Question: 他很忙吗?(Is he busy?)

**Positive-negative form** (A-not-A):
- 贵不贵?(Is it expensive or not?)
- 好不好?(Is it good or not?)
- 冷不冷?(Is it cold or not?)

Note: The positive-negative form omits 很.

## [phase:integration] Nuanced understanding of 很

### question singleChoice difficulty:4
tags: phase:integration, pragmatics
In natural conversation, a speaker says 这个贵 (without 很) after looking at several items. Then later says 那个很贵 about a different item. What is the speaker likely communicating?
a: Both items are equally expensive
b: The first item is more expensive than others seen; the second is just stating a fact about it being expensive
c: The first item is cheap; the second is expensive

explanation: Without 很, 这个贵 implies comparison to other items (this one is MORE expensive). With 很, 那个很贵 makes a more neutral observation, though context may add emphasis. The key is recognizing the comparative function of bare adjectives versus the neutral function of 很 + adjective.
