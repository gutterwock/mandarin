# Mandarin Chinese: Zero to Mastery (HSK 1–6)

**Goal:** Progress from zero Mandarin to advanced mastery (HSK 6) through structured HSK-aligned courses and vocabulary-focused companion courses built around authentic Chinese media and literature.
**Prerequisites:** None
**Estimated scope:** 13 courses, 3 projects, ~900–1,100 study hours

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Read and write simplified Chinese characters up to HSK 6 level (~5,000+ characters)
- Produce and distinguish all four tones accurately in natural speech
- Communicate fluently in Mandarin on everyday, professional, and abstract topics at HSK 5+ level
- Read and comprehend authentic Chinese texts — literary, journalistic, and contemporary fiction
- Analyse authentic Chinese media for vocabulary, cultural references, and linguistic patterns
- Navigate register variation — spoken vernacular, formal written, literary, and classical-influenced (成语)
- Deploy complex grammar from basic sentence patterns (HSK 1) through nuanced discourse markers and literary register (HSK 6)

---

## Notes

**Script and tones:** Mandarin is maximally distant from English — different script, tonal phonology, and radically different grammar. Tones and pinyin are foundational: without them the learner cannot approximate pronunciation from text. Both are introduced at HSK 1 before any vocabulary content. Hanzi are introduced alongside pinyin from HSK 1; the program does not take a pinyin-only or characters-delayed approach.

**Character progression:** Characters accumulate across all levels — ~300 at HSK 1–2, ~600 at HSK 3, ~1,200 at HSK 4, ~2,500 at HSK 5, ~5,000+ at HSK 6. Active spaced-repetition character review should run alongside all courses throughout the program.

**Media courses** (Stages 5, 6, 8, 9, 12, 14, 15) do not teach plot, characters, or story. The learner reads or watches the work independently. The course's role is purely linguistic:
- Pre-teach vocabulary, idioms, and cultural expressions that appear in the work before quizzing
- Questions test language comprehension — not narrative comprehension
- No plot summaries, character introductions, or thematic analysis

**Concurrent media options:** Stages 5–6 (*Rickshaw Boy* and *Popular Songs*), Stages 8–9 (*The Outcast* and *All Is Well*), and Stages 14–15 (*Blood Merchant* and *Three-Body Problem*) can be studied in either order or concurrently — they share the same prerequisite level and are independent of each other.

**Writing projects** (Stages 3, 10, 16) are longer-form production exercises evaluated separately by an LLM. They are not part of the tutor study queue — the learner completes them independently.

**Format limitations:** From HSK 5 onward, structured courses in this format are unlikely to be sufficient for actual fluency on their own. Real fluency requires extensive immersion — reading authentic Chinese texts, watching Chinese media without subtitles, and conversational practice with native speakers.

## Stages

---

## Stage 1: Mandarin HSK 1 — Absolute Beginner

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk1`
Scope: Absolute beginner Mandarin — the four tones and tone sandhi rules; pinyin romanisation; introduction to simplified hanzi; greetings, numbers, basic nouns and verbs; survival phrases and formulaic expressions. Vocabulary to ~300 words. Covers HSK 1.
Prerequisites: None
Estimated subtopics: ~40

---

## Stage 2: Mandarin HSK 2 — Elementary

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk2`
Scope: Elementary Mandarin — daily routines, simple exchanges, time and place expressions, basic question forms, measure words, aspect markers (了, 过, 着), simple negation, basic serial verb constructions. Vocabulary to ~600 words. Covers HSK 2.
Prerequisites: Stage 1
Estimated subtopics: ~40

---

## Stage 3: Basic Communicator Project

Type: Project
Scope: Write a self-introduction entirely in Mandarin (with pinyin) covering: your name, age, nationality, occupation, and daily routine. Then write responses to five simple questions about yourself (e.g. "你叫什么名字？", "你每天几点起床？"). Each response should be 1–3 sentences. Use only vocabulary and structures from HSK 1–2. Assessed on: correct measure words, basic sentence patterns (S+V+O), time expressions, and deployment of learned vocabulary.
Prerequisites: Stage 2
Estimated duration: ~1–2 weeks
Outcomes:
- Complete self-introduction in Mandarin demonstrating HSK 1–2 grammar and vocabulary
- Accurate use of measure words, sentence patterns, and time expressions

---

## Stage 4: Mandarin HSK 3 — Pre-Intermediate

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk3`
Scope: Pre-intermediate Mandarin — expressing opinions and experiences, simple narratives, result and directional complements, 把-construction, comparison patterns (比, 没有, 一样), expanded vocabulary for work, travel, and daily life. Vocabulary to ~900 words. Covers HSK 3.
Prerequisites: Stage 2
Estimated subtopics: ~45

---

## Stage 5: *Rickshaw Boy* — Lao She (Graded Reader)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-rickshaw-boy`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from the graded reader edition of *骆驼祥子* (*Rickshaw Boy*, Lao She). Language of early 20th-century Beijing working life — labour, poverty, urban vernacular, period-specific vocabulary. Pre-teaches vocabulary and cultural expressions from the text before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 6.
Prerequisites: Stage 4
Estimated subtopics: ~25

---

## Stage 6: Mandarin Popular Songs — Media Companion

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-popular-songs`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from Mandarin popular music (流行音乐). Lyrical language, emotional expression, and poetic vocabulary across love songs, nostalgic ballads, youth anthems, and contemporary C-pop. Pre-teaches vocabulary and expressions from selected songs before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 5.
Prerequisites: Stage 4
Estimated subtopics: ~25

---

## Stage 7: Mandarin HSK 4 — Intermediate

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk4`
Scope: Intermediate Mandarin — abstract topics, longer discourse, formal and informal register distinctions, complex sentence structures (虽然…但是, 不仅…而且, 尽管…还是), potential complements, pivotal sentences, passive with 被. Vocabulary to ~1,200 words. Covers HSK 4.
Prerequisites: Stage 4
Estimated subtopics: ~50

---

## Stage 8: *The Outcast* — *一人之下* (Manhua)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-the-outcast`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from the manhua *一人之下* (*The Outcast*). Daoist terminology, folk religion vocabulary, martial arts register, and contemporary slang that appear in the series. Pre-teaches vocabulary and cultural expressions before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 9.
Prerequisites: Stage 7
Estimated subtopics: ~25

---

## Stage 9: *All Is Well* — *都挺好* (TV Series, 2019)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-all-is-well`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from *都挺好* (*All Is Well*). Language of contemporary urban family drama — conflict, obligation, reconciliation, filial piety vocabulary, modern Chinese family discourse. Pre-teaches vocabulary and expressions from the series before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 8.
Prerequisites: Stage 7
Estimated subtopics: ~25

---

## Stage 10: Independent Reader and Speaker Project

Type: Project
Scope: Find a short authentic Chinese text (a news headline plus a 2–3 paragraph article from a Chinese news source). Write a 150-character response in Mandarin expressing your opinion on the topic, using at least two connective expressions (e.g. 虽然…但是, 不仅…而且). Separately, write a 100+ character first-person account of a real personal experience (a trip, a meal, an event), narrated in the past tense. Assessed on: ability to process authentic input, express a stance with reasoning, narrate a sequence of events, and shift register appropriately.
Prerequisites: Stage 7
Estimated duration: ~1–2 weeks
Outcomes:
- Demonstrate HSK 4-level reading comprehension of authentic Chinese text
- Produce structured opinion writing and personal narrative using HSK 4 grammar

---

## Stage 11: Mandarin HSK 5 — Upper Intermediate

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk5`
Scope: Upper-intermediate to advanced Mandarin — academic and professional contexts, complex grammar patterns (固然, 以…为, 不免, 未必, 何况), extended reading and writing production, formal written register, idiomatic expression, discourse markers for argumentation. Vocabulary to ~2,500 words. Covers HSK 5.
Prerequisites: Stage 7
Estimated subtopics: ~55

---

## Stage 12: *To Live* — Yu Hua (1993)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-to-live`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from *活着* (*To Live*, Yu Hua). Literary and historical vocabulary of 20th-century Chinese rural life — land reform, Cultural Revolution, famine; vernacular narrative register; Yu Hua's plain but emotionally dense prose. Pre-teaches the highest-value vocabulary from the text before quizzing.
Prerequisites: Stage 11
Estimated subtopics: ~35

---

## Stage 13: Mandarin HSK 6 — Advanced

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-hsk6`
Scope: Advanced to mastery Mandarin — nuanced expression across formal and literary registers; idiomatic fluency; classical Chinese references (成语 chéngyǔ); sophisticated written production; complex discourse structures; connotative and register precision. Vocabulary to ~5,000+ words. Covers HSK 6.
Prerequisites: Stage 11
Estimated subtopics: ~55

---

## Stage 14: *Chronicle of a Blood Merchant* — Yu Hua (1995)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-blood-merchant`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from *许三观卖血记* (*Chronicle of a Blood Merchant*, Yu Hua). Vernacular rhythms, dark humour, repetition as stylistic device, historical vocabulary of rural China; contrasts with *To Live* in register and tone. Pre-teaches the highest-value vocabulary from the text before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 15.
Prerequisites: Stage 13
Estimated subtopics: ~35

---

## Stage 15: *The Three-Body Problem* — Liu Cixin (2008)

Type: Course
Course ID: `mandarin/mandarin-media-three-body-problem`
Scope: Vocabulary, idioms, and cultural references from *三体* (*The Three-Body Problem*, Liu Cixin). Scientific and philosophical vocabulary, Cultural Revolution historical language, contemporary technical register, cosmological and physics terminology. Pre-teaches the highest-value vocabulary from the text before quizzing. Can be done concurrently with Stage 14.
Prerequisites: Stage 13
Estimated subtopics: ~35

---

## Stage 16: Fluent Practitioner Project

Type: Project
Scope: Write a 500-character essay responding to a social or cultural question — for example: 城市化对中国家庭结构的影响 (the impact of urbanisation on Chinese family structure), 科技与孤独感 (technology and loneliness), or a question of your own choosing at equivalent depth. Draw on vocabulary and register from HSK 5–6 material and at least one of the companion texts (*Blood Merchant*, *Three-Body Problem*, or *To Live*). Assessed on: sustained argument across multiple paragraphs, varied and complex sentence structures, appropriate literary or formal register, idiomatic expression, and ability to reference cultural context in support of an argument.
Prerequisites: Stage 13
Estimated duration: ~3–5 weeks
Outcomes:
- Completed 500-character essay demonstrating HSK 6-level grammar and register
- Sustained argumentation drawing on literary and cultural vocabulary from companion texts
